D6 Mechanics¶

Two kinds of rolls: Skill Tests and Effect Magnitudes.

  • For a skill test, state an action and the skill being used.

    • GM has a standard difficulty threshold or an opposition difficulty from a character opposing the action.

    • GM may have difficulty modifiers for setting and situation, also.

  1. Gather Dice, nD+m. One die will be the Odd-colored “Wild Die”.

    For a Skill Test, look at attributes, skills, special abilities in use. Don’t forget advantages and disadvantages.

    For an Effect Magnitude, look at the weapon, skill, special ability for the number of dice. GM may include modifiers based on skill test results, setting, situation, or opposition.

  1. Spend a Fate Point to Roll double the dice.

  2. Roll.

    Wild Die Adjustments¶

    Wild Die

    Result

    1

    Failure. Discard wild die and highest number die.

    6

    Success. Roll the wild die again. Accumulate values; stop when a non-6 is rolled.

  3. Spend a Character Point to add a die.

  4. Result.

    • For a Skill Test, two kinds of results:

      • Skill roll ≥ Difficulty: success of some kind. See Result Points; this may impact the effect magnitude.

      • Skill roll < Difficulty: failure.

    • For Effect Magnitude this is the body points, or duration, or range, etc. See `Damage`_ for a common case.
