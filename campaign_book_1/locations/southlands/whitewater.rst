City of White Water
===================

The City of White Water is adjacent to the last waterfall in the river.
From here to the coast, the land is flat, leaving the river to meander through low hills to the coastal marshes.

This is a boom-town, it's fortunes been rising since the King has been unable to defend the northern coast against pirates.
The city doesn't (currently) have walls and only a few gate towers.

The river pours over the cliffs with primary drop, of about 2 meters, into a long, narrow pool -- something over 50 meters -- filled with boulders and rapids.
The end of the pool is an abrupt turn to the south, where the river exits the gorge, dropping another 3 meters.
Here it becomes wider and flatter and wanders aimlessly through the farmland, seeking a path to the sea.

The river and the cliffs divide the city into four sections:

-   Above the falls on the right bank (the west side of the river) is where caravan traffic arrives and departs.
    The taverns and stables cater to these clients.

-   Above the falls on the left bank (the east side) is farmland.
    There are few roads here, and few destinations.

-   Below the falls on the right bank is where river traffic unloads.
    The warehouses and taverns here cater to the "River Rats" who sail (and pole) barges up and down the river.

-   Below the falls on the left bank is also farmland.
    These farmers struggle with the relatively poor-quality sandy soil.


The main roads through town converge on a broad flight of steps called "The Ladder" between the upper and lower parts of the city.
A few businesses are located on either side of the ladder. Life converges here, and the steps are crowded with market stalls.
