Act of Desperation
==============================

:Location: The party's preferred hangout. See :ref:`character.party`.

:Date: 15 Summer

:Time: Mid-morning

White Water's High Knight, Sir Bond, should arrive today, on midsummer, to visit City of Eagles.

(The mage temple didn't add an Extra Day this year; chopping Midsummer into a single-day holiday.
It's a bit odd that Sir Bond didn't consult with priests and depart a day or two earlier.)


Context
----------

Ordinarily, your friends would start drinking on the Extra Day.
Early in the morning, you'd ride up to a pond in the hills to do some fishing for a serious midsummer feast.
The lack of an Extra Day for the preparation has left you wondering what to do with yourselves.

You have a cozy relationship with Squire Angler, Captain of the Guard of City of Eagles.
You've helped each other out in the past.
He's even invited some of you fishing at his secret spot under a waterfall not far into the forest.

..  only:: source

    He needs to contact you before White Water arrives.
    To be sure that there is no trouble, he would like loyal followers to be around tonight to suppress any "difficulties".

    And there are worse troubles brewing.  The Baron of White Water is coming.  The visit has been plagued by problems.  Spies everywhere.  Guards disappearing.  Secrets leaked.  It's awful, and he doesn't know where to turn.  You're relatively unknown around the knight's castle, could you help?

    When he makes contact, he is followed by spies of Xorn who will seek to neutralize you.

    You and your party have decided to skip out on all of your other duties and go fishing.  An old friend of yours, the Captain of the Guard of the City of Eagles stops by with an important message.  When the Captain of the City of Eagles stops by, he was tailed.  The thugs tailing him are assigned to kill any contacts he makes.  You're the target.

Complication
---------------------

(If they're outside, they'll hear the horse as the squire approaches.
If they're inside, they'll hear the rattle of armor from Squire Angler.)

Squire Angler looks around, as if he expects that he was followed.
His greeting is little more than grinding his teeth.
He's traveling around fairly heavily armed; it's not full battlefield harness, but it's the essential pauldrons, greaves, and gauntlets.

(Roll a difficulty Moderate 11 to compare Squire's attitude with other times you've met him.)

-   **Success**. No one has ever seen him this worried before.
    Indeed, no one can recall seeing him worried before.
    He earned his name as a happy-go-lucky fisherman who happens to be really good at locating threats hidden in ponds and streams.
    That baiting a lure skill is how he became the High Knight's spymaster.
    (It's also possible he reports directly to Earl Hillshire, and is merely assigned to City of Eagles.)

-   **Failure**. He's often pretty gloomy.
    It's his job to see the glass as half-full.

"Our earl asked me to shoulder a serious problem," he announces.
"And my mother told me more than once, the best way to carry a burden is to share it."

You both know about the upsurge in the activities of bandits and highwaymen.
The bandits have made the road into a war zone.

With the prevailing problems in the kingdom, you also agree that Earl Hillshire hasn't put any effort to keeping the roads safe.
Hillshire has publicly blamed Southlands for failing to keep merchants safe.
Sir Warrant has become responsible for a long stretch of road, with few resources.

Blaming Southlands, the squire observes, flies in the face of what everyone can see.
Caravans that get down out of the hills intact always make it to White Water.
Caravans that get into trouble become a burden to the border villages or the big royal tavern near the border.

Anyone who talks with caravan operators or merchants, knows this is a Hillshire problem.

What's troubling the squire today is the arrival of the knight -- Sir Bond -- from White Water.
He's got several points:

-   There are rumors Sir Bond is bringing his daughter, and -- perhaps -- a dowry offer.
    This is something bandits would like to take to prevent further negotiations.

-   There's a rumor the Temple of the Jackal -- up in the hills somewhere -- might have started buying axes and hammers.
    Either they're behind this, or they're going to leverage the situation to escalate the banditry.

It's hard for the Squire to be sure, but it sounds like there is more than one group looking to create trouble.
The rumors come from a paid informant who's got a kind of "relationship" with some of the bandits.
Squire makes it plain: paid informants will say anything for money; without confirmation, it's difficult to act on this rumor.

Squire Angler's pitch to the party is this: Sir Warrant needs information.
Angler's too well known to gather intelligence in person.
The Party is not as well known and might even be able to get inside the bandit or temple network, and figure out who these bandits are and what they're going to do.

..  admonition:: GM Notes on Bribes

    A token bribe among friends is often 2-3 G, or sometimes 20 S.
    This is culturally common, and reinforces the social network among people.
    This gives them a one day boost of +1D to a Funds roll.
    Squire Angler's bag is about 6 of these +1D boosts that the characters can allocate.

    A bribe for potentially dangerous work is 4-6 G, or 40 S, a temporary bump of bump of +2D to funds.

    A very serious bribe is 10 G (80 S): a temporary bump of +8D to the target's Funds.

    See the Tavern Food Costs table for the scope of a banquet one gets for 6-7 S.
    Travelers and outsiders often have a +1 difficulty on purchases, or need to pay a full G (8 S).

To sweeten the deal, Angler's got a bag of about 10 G and 20 S to invest in bribes at the taverns.
(See :external:ref:`fantasy.equipment` for price details.)

Crisis
-------

"You'll need to work fast --" Squire Angler begins.
Then arrows start raining in...

Anyone with Combat Sense may detect the presence of attackers rushing the place.

One Hired Thug Leader and four Bandit Army Hired Thugs start shooting.

Climax
-------

They're out for blood. Negotiation -- while not impossible -- requires you to stop them from shooting before they'll listen.

..  admonition:: GM Note

    If party deaths look imminent, there are two sources of help that can intervene:

    -   Butcher.

    -   Stitches.

    Generally, Butcher should show up just after the nick of time.
    Some kinds of help Butcher can offer:

    -   Chasing down any escaping thugs.

    -   Take the party to Stitches who will help to patch up the survivors.


Consequences
------------

If a thug is caught and questioned, they know very little, except what lies they were told by someone who hangs out at the *Fox's Tail*, a tavern down by the river.

Squire Angler takes this as proof there will be yet more troubles plaguing this state visit.
Meet him at the knight's castle no later than the start of Dog Watch (8 PM) that night.

A local butcher, called Butcher, who's also some kind of bounty-hunter, will arrive.
He can get the characters to a Priest, called Stitches, outside the Hælan temple who can heal injuries.
He's unhappy taking you to a temple, they're too well watched.
He's been tailing the lead ruffian, a known trouble-maker, who might be in the Jackal cult.

Butcher takes you to a drunken former Haelan priest, called Stitches.
He can help you when you get hurt.
He's a friend of Angler, and keeps moving from place to place to keep off the radar of enemies of White Water.

Butcher has been paying a man to watch the *Fox's Tail*.  He can't go in there himself, as it might reveal his interest in Temple of the Jackal thugs.
