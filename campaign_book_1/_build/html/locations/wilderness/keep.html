Temple of the Cult of the Jackal¶

Concealed in a wide, flat hollow, high in the hills, lies this obscure structure. It’s neither large, nor imposing. It’s not designed to intimidate by hulking on the edge of a steep hill, and providing a commanding view of a valley. It’s nestled deep in the hills, designed to remain as a secure, hidden retreat, surrounded by higher peaks.

Note

Jackals are not part of this continent’s wildlife.

Somewhere on the continent to the North, there’s a place with a Jackal-headed god, who looks like the Egyptian Anubis.

People who are casually aware of the cult often substitute “wolf” for “jackal” because that’s an animal they know. Traders and travelers in the western continent may use “coyote”, because it makes them sound more worldly.

The true followers never make a mistake. To them, the term “jackal” becomes a kind of passcode; something outsiders wouldn’t know.

The temple’s valley is a bit less than a kilometer long in the E-W direction. A number of little streams in rocky channels converge in a pond, and drop over a fall to a lower, narrower valley. In the N-S direction, it’s a bit less than a half-kilometer wide.

The location of the Temple is a closely-guarded secret. Pilgrims need directions, and must contact one of the spies in Eagles to get directions and the password.

The most effective entry is to get captured by the bandit army and dragged up here.

General¶

The structure is just under 140 m along the north-facing wall. The western wall is 60 m long. The eastern wall is 40 m long. The southern wall has two turns in it: walking the top is 162 m. The total circuit, marched by the guards is a hair over 400m.

GM Note

Given a typical character’s Move value of 10m/r, it’s possible for an officer to hustle around the wall in about four minutes. Since there’s no reason to hurry, a typical circuit takes about 12-16 minutes, with long pauses at each tower to stare into the distance.

As a general guideline – if a character makes a simple Acumen-related observation skill roll – use 4D minutes as the time for a circuit by a guard.

There are a few obvious structures that comprise the larger temple structure.

  • Wall. The compound wall is crenelated, only 8-10m high, with five small towers in the corners. The visible force is about 30 men. The towers are 10m high.

    The rumor about other-worldly goblins patrolling the forest around the keep after nightfall is true.

  • Gate House. The Gate House is 8-9m high, with a flat roof, and crenelation, allowing armed archers to shoot down into the road. It has a double door, which open during the day. The front has archer slits that house some guards with cross-bows, plus the keeper of the gate. Inside the gatehouse are spiral stairs leading to the roof.

    A concealed door (difficulty Easy 8, to discover because it’s heavily used) reveals stairs descending into a small basement.

    Pilgrims are admitted with a little discussion beyond some secret phrases or gestures.

    Hirelings and merchants with supplies must present paperwork to be granted access to the temple.

  • The New Keep. The New Keep is the cult’s nerve center. It houses the human guards, as well as his planning staff and intelligence gatherers. It has the main dungeons, as well as some magical equipment for spying out the doings in various cities.

  • The Central Tower. The Central Tower is the Temple of the Jackal. Religious ceremonies and magical procedures are performed here. The central nave is open for five stories, and occasionally has birds and what-not flying around.

    The forest is ancient, and does have trees taller than this tower. From the cleared road, and the surrounding hills, the tower is prominent because it erupts out of a large clearing.

  • The Old Keep. The Old Keep is an ancient building with old-style haphazard stone work. It has been added-to and extended over the centuries.

    While it once was the main building, it is now the private residence of Xorn and his main henchmen.

  • Barn and Stables. Up against the north wall are two small wooden structures. They have the usual features of barns and stables: horses, manure, farming tools, hay.

    Outside the barn is a 30m × 4m garden plot with some herbs and vegetables.

  • The Lodges. There are two outside lodges for Pilgrims: called the East Lodge and the West Lodge. These are 14m by 20m buildings. They’re two stories above ground, with exposed foundations suggesting a basement. (They’re not like common rural farmhouses built over the animal pen.)

Less obvious are two caves. The East Cave and South Cave are snuggled in deep, dry ravines, hidden by piles of boulders. With some careful searching (difficulty Moderate 14) the caves can be found. The ravines in which the caves are concealed are suspiciously dry; the water flows down a gulley not far away. The caves are inhabited by a horde of otherworldy goblins.

It’s not clear precisely how a horde of goblins were pulled into this realm, or for what purpose. It’s not even clear when they might have arrived; they may be marooned here.

Characters¶

There are three distinct forces within the Temple.

  • The Cult of the Jackal. This is a force composed of clerics, novices, and pilgrims. This group is the base for the externally focused intelligence operation in City of Eagles and City of White Water.

  • The Bandit Army with mages and various thugs and criminals.

  • There is also a horde of Goblins that sometimes infests the temple valley.

The Cult of the Jackal is the religious arm.

Gate House¶

The road leads up to the gate house. To it’s west, there are two lodges, outside the wall, and seemingly undefended.

The gate is flanked by walls with a few arrow slits to allow archers to shoot down on someone approaching the gate.

The outermost door – made of heavy timbers, iron straps, and massive studs – appears to never close. There are weeds growing around it.

The inner portcullis is raised and lowered every day.

  1. Two-story entrance tunnel. (About 7m high.) There’s an outer door, an inner iron bar portcullis, and beyond that, an inner door.

The Underkeep¶

Before the Gatehouse and Walls (which are old) there was the Old Keep. Before the Old Keep, there was something else here, which was buried and forgotten, rebuilt, torn down, buried and forgotten a second time.

There are two distinct parts to the Underkeep, the Old portion is mostly under the the current locations of the Old Keep and Gate House. The New portion is mostly under the Central Tower. The terms old and new are relative to each other, of course; all of the surface construction is newer than the new portion of the Underkeep.

Careful study (difficulty Moderate 15, with access to the Old portion of the underkeep) can reveal the Old Keep was built from rubble of an even older construction that once stood here. Further, the part underlying the Old Keep is relatively small, with only 5 distinct rooms.

To the west, under the Central Tower is a distinct Underkeep. This is newer, and far more confusing.

There’s a connection between them: a hidden door between 40.2 and 40.3. The design of the catch that holds the door closed is awkwardly high, and requires a great deal of leverage to operate. The difficulty Moderate 14 to find the hidden recess. Operating the catch is a difficulty Difficult 16. Taking time to study it, and improvising some tools will reduce this to a more a 12.

  3. Four junior officers sleep here. Chests are clothes and armor. There are 2 × 5D gold and 4 × 5D silver coins hidden in various places.

  4. Four senior officers sleep here. Chests are clothes and armor. There are 3 × 5D gold and 5 × 5D silver coins hidden in various places. These offices know the truth: they’re unable to return to their realm.

  5. Torture area. Disgusting torture instruments.

  6. Another torture area. More disgusting torture instruments.

  7. Two guards who don’t seem to care. The cells aren’t well made.

Level -3¶

  1. Goblin Great Leader’s bedroom. 1D females here. 200 GS. Three weird goblin jewelry per female. Sometimes called a Duke or a King. No one really knows how social ranking works for them.

  2. Goblin Leader’s Study. The open book is incomprehensible goblin scratching. One of the other books is a kind of dictionary from the older language spoken by the Outlanders and Goblin.

    With appropriate language skills (difficulty Moderate 12) one can find what appears to be an enchantment to close the veil.

    With deeper language skills (difficulty Moderate 15) one can find the enchantment to open the veil, but it appears oddly incomplete.

  3. Goblin Leader’s Servants/Guards. Four goblins.

  4. Goblin Leader’s court and throne.

  5. Rec area 1. 1D goblins here, gaming.

  6. Mess area. 2D goblins here, eating. Kitchen against E wall.

  7. Rec area 2. 1D goblins here, drinking.

  8. Junk. Shovels, picks, brooms, buckets, torches, chain, rope, etc.

  9. More Junk. Shelves, cabinets, bed frames, etc.

  10. Two goblin guards here to act as messengers.

South Cave¶

About 80m from the tower in the Southwest corner of the keep, on the sloping side of a boulder-strewn hillside, there’s the carefully hidden South Cave.

The spoor of raiding Goblins is often cleaned away; it’s difficulty Moderate 15 to notice the traces.
