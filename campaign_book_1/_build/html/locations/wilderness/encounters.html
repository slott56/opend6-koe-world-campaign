Encounters¶

The Temple of the Jackal is a one-day horseback ride north and East of City of Eagles. Of course, the route must be known by someone in the party or the party must have a guide. Otherwise, it will be much more difficult.

For a party without a guide (or any experience), finding the correct path has a difficulty of Heroic for any Tracking skill roll.

If the party fails the difficulty check for finding the correct path then the party will not make it to the Temple in a single day.

An unguided party in Hillshire will suffer from two problems:

  • Pursuit by a company of bandits during the day. See The Bandit Army. They are not particularly stealthy. They are skilled at tracking. (See the character sheets, below.) If the party tries to conceal themselves, then the bandirs will make an opposed difficulty roll with the best tracking skill of the bandits.

  • Attack by a horde of goblins after dark. See The Goblin Hordes. They are the opposite of stealthy. They are moderately skilled at tracking. If the party tries to conceal themselves, then the goblins will make an opposed difficulty roll with a tracking skill of 3D.

For a party with a guide (or some experience with the road to the Temple), the ordinary wilderness creatures that frequent these hills will prey on a party. It’s a difficulty of Moderate on a Tracking skill roll to avoid monsters in the wilderness. Failure of this roll means picking a monster from the Wilderness Encounters table.

The border with Southlands is a three-day horseback ride on the road. Encounters are less likely, since there are royal Taverns spaced about 16km apart.

On the road, it’s a difficulty of Easy on a Tracking skill roll to avoid monsters. Failure of this roll means picking a monster from the Wilderness Encounters table.

The Bandit Army¶

The Bandit Army is loosely associated with the Temple of the Jackal. Currently, they are based in the Temple

GM Note

The bandits are not delighted to be living in the Temple of the Jackal.

(Difficulty Very Difficult.) The party is able to talk to the General (or the Colonel mage) before someone kills you. The Bandit Army is not open to negotiation. A show of force can reduce the difficulty by -4 to -6 depending on how overwhelming the force is.

(Difficulty Difficult.) The party is able to convince the General (or the Colonel mage) to split from the Cult of the Jackal. The Temple is low-rent.

The army has 42 men-at-arms. See New Keep Second Level. These are split into 3 companies. Each company will be 12 men plus 2 officers.

Travel through Hillshire during daylight risks an encounter with a bandit company of 14.

GM Note

The Temple has scrying devices, and actively uses divination powers to locate anyone within 10 km of the Temple.

If nothing is done to conceal the party, they will be met by company of 12 men at about 5 km from the temple.

If the party does make an effort to conceal themselves, then this becomes an opposed difficulty between mages of the Temple and the party.

See Temple of the Cult of the Jackal for the Divination mages and the scrying devices available.

The Goblin Hordes¶

The Goblins are trapped in this realm. They were summoned and do not have the power to return. They live in caves associated with the Temple. See South Cave and East Cave.

(And another cave, two more days ride into the wilderness. See The Jackal’s Secret Cave.)

The Temple caves have about 80 goblins. Each cave has two hordes of 20, that take different parts of the night hours to raid.

Travel through Hillshire after dark risks an encounter with a goblin horde of 20.
