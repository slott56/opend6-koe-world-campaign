The Rod is Lost
==============================

:Location: Sir Warrant's Castle to start.
    Optionally, any of the following:

    -   :ref:`location.eagles.foxstail` for the details of the tavern and people found there.

    -   :ref:`location.eagles.theoaks` for the details of the riding stable.

    -   :ref:`location.eagles.library` for the details of the book store.

    -   :ref:`location.eagles.thirstycrow` for the details of the *Thirsty Crow* tavern.


:Date: 15 Summer

:Time: Dog Watch (8PM to Midnight) until Forenoon the next day.

..  structure: context complication crisis climax (i.e., battle) consequences.

Context
-------

The Knight from White Water, Sir Bond, arrived earlier today to renew the marriage negotiation.
This is a breakthrough in the negotiations, since Sir Bond is paranoid of spies and assassination.
He will only travel with a heavily armed retinue.

Sir Bond brought some dowry items, mostly valuable trinkets: some furs, a few items of jewelry.
Secretly, however, he has also brought the powerful **Lightning Rod**.  Squire Neck, captain of the guard, is personally responsible for the rod.

Currently, there's a reception in the Earl's Castle.

When the party arrives they're taken to the servant's entrance and shown to Squire Angler's quarters.
His rooms are a dark, unpleasant place with a few chairs for some of the party to sit.
Refreshments are out of the question, since all the servants are busy.
Angler hasn't had time to explain the situation to the party when a servant runs in with news.

Complication
------------

During the dancing after dinner, a groom ran in, looking for help.
Squire Starter -- Son of Sir Warrant of Eagles -- is  fighting with White Water's Guard Captain, Squire Neck, in the stables.
There's a dead man there, too.
The groom isn't prepared to get involved in a fight among armed squires.

By the time Sir Warrant and Sir Bond arrive,
Squire Neck has fled from the stables, leaving Squire Starter slumped against a wall, bruised and confused.

Squire Starter has too many confused stories to trust his sanity.
One is about having a house guard tell him about a band of thieves in the stables.
Another story is that he heard a scuffle over the noise of the reception.
A third wild tale is that he had a suspicion of thieves and was afraid they might be here to assassinate Sir Bond.

He claims he found Squire Neck helping the thieves to make off with Lightning Rod.
There's a dead body in the stable, however, clearly killed by the Squire Neck's bloody mace.
This makes the "helping" part of Squire Starter's accusation against Squire Neck look weak.

There's no explanation for why Squire Neck was in the stables and not at the reception.
None of the men-at-arms were told anything.
The two that saw him leave thought he was going to the privy, since he said nothing.

Sir Warrant doesn't seem likely to order someone to  steal the **Lightning Rod**. First, it's a gift. Second, he needs a negotiated peace with increased trade.
Stealing the dowry that came with Oven turns an awkward negotiation into grounds for a local war.
This would put a halt to successful marriage between Squire Starter and Oven.

One possible explanation for the burglary is Sir Bond has some kind of contrived plot to create an excuse for war.
He could have ordered one of his own people steal his own artifact.
He would be forced to hide it -- and couldn't use it in the coming battle.
The dead body makes this complicated scenario unlikely.

It gets worse, of course.

With Squire Neck missing, Squire Starter has put his father, Sir Warrant in two awkward positions.

-   Blaming his son seems logical. It makes sense to back his own children. But. His son doesn't seem to have a clear idea of what happened; this makes Sir Warrant look like he really is guilty of something.

-   Blaming Squire Neck, and declaring him outlaw seems logical. An outsider is easy to blame. But why would Squire Neck wait until he was in City of Eagles to steal something he could steal in City of White Water?

Crisis
------

Of course, the reception ended with shouting and folks waving weapons around.

Oven, Sir Bond's daughter, and Squire Starter of Eagles have managed to have some private conversations.

If Oven's father, Sir Bond, makes any public accusations over the missing **Lightning Rod**, the wealthy nobility of White Water will label him a fool for losing a powerful artifact.
It's worth a lot of money, and -- in a way -- as dowry, it's going to be hers. She wants it.
She says her father thinks his best path forward is to complain about the paltry offer for marriage, lax security, and storm off in a huff.

Squire Starter reports that Oven suggests they find the Rod before Earl Southlands or Earl Hillshire find out.
Having a White Waters artifact stolen while negotiating with City of Eagles proves that someone is unstrustworthy.
For Earl Southlands, this will be proof that City of Eagles is a den of thieves and bandits.
For Earl Hillshire, it might be proof that City of White Water -- and by extension all of Southlands -- can't be trusted.


Climax
--------

Butcher (see :ref:`chapter.foxs_tail`) will recognize the body as yet another hired ruffian from the Fox's Tail.
He and Squire Angler think a visit to the Fox's Tail is in order.

Angler needs to know what happened in the stables.
Why where Squire Starter and Squire Neck there?
Why did Squire Starter attach Squire Neck?
Where is Squire Neck?

There are several parts to this scenario's climax.

Some can be done right away, others need to be deferred.
While Angler needs information, he's willing to put the party up for the night in the castle's front room (inside the eastern door) so they can get an early start the next morning.

(Squire Angler lives nearby, but his house is too small for the party.)

1.  15 Summer (later at night). Investigate Sir Warrant's stables.

    The External Spying operations run by the Temple of the Jackal are behind Squire Starter's confusion.

    Someone with outstanding mage skills can penetrate the mystery around Squire Starter.
    A skill roll with a difficulty of Difficult for a character with  *scholarship* and an Extranormal skill may reveal the mechanism in some detail.

    **Success**. Knowing about paired tokens provides a path to investigate at the stables.

        ..  admonition:: GM Note

            Mages prepare pairs of tokens to be passed to the various parties. This relies on establishing a link using two coin-like silver pieces that are -- the extent possible -- identical.
            A mage will have one, and use the other as a way to link to the person holding it; they think of this as one thing in two places, a very strong link.

            In this case, bandit mages were camped nearby.
            Local pickpockets were hired to pass the tokens.

            One pair of tokens was used to control a member of the White Water party. The coin-like silver piece was passed to Squire Neck in the marketplace.
            The other pair of tokens was passed to Squire Starter by someone who was supposed to be mucking out the stables.

            Squire Neck had stashed the coin deep in his saddlebag. Since he wasn't near the coin, there was no link for the enchantment, and he arrived **during** the burglary.

            Squire Starter, however, kept the foreign-looking coin in the coin pouch on his belt.
            This worked, the mages confused him, and he has no idea what's going on.

        An astute mage may be able to use divination to detect the presence of the enchanted tokens. The Cult of the Jackal mages have taken steps to hide themselves. The outcome of the divination attempt is opposed by a difficulty of Very Difficult.

        -   **Success**. Squire Starter's token is readily found: it's in his purse. Squire Neck's token is in the tack room, under guard by Sir Bond's men. It will be clear there are only two pairs of tokens.

        -   **Failure**. No other tokens can be found. Nor can the mage be sure how many token pairs are involved.

        Given Squire Starter or Squire Neck's tokens, the difficulty of following the link has a difficulty of Difficult. The enchantments are concealed by opposing glamors.

        -   **Success**. The matching token are in a grove of trees outside the wall. Not far from *The Oaks* Riding Stable.

        -   **Failure**. The matching token cannot be located.

        ..  include:: ../characters/jackal_external.txt

    **Failure**. No one knows about paired tokens or how Squire Starter was left so utterly confused.


2.  Investigate  *The Fox's Tail*.
    This can be done the night of 15 Summer, or, the morning of 16 summer.

    -   If the party never went to *The Fox's Tail* earlier, move to :ref:`chapter.foxs_tail`. Modify the scenario to remove any delay getting to talk to the barkeeper, since the dinner rush is over.

    -   If the party already went to *The Fox's Tail*, but left quickly, learning little, and avoiding the thugs, also move to :ref:`chapter.foxs_tail`, modifying the scenario to allow for being much later in the evening.

    -   If the party went to *The Fox's Tail*, interrogated the Bartender, and got waylaid by thugs, going back will be a problem.
        They will get mugged by yet *another* band of ruffians. This may also include any drunks that feel like throwing fists around.

        This band of ruffians will be lead by the Spy from *The Oaks* riding stable as well as the Spy Henchman.
        See :ref:`location.eagles.theoaks` for details on the ruffians that hang out by the stables.

3.  Spend the night in Sir Warrant's front hall. (It's cramped and drafty, but it's also free, and comes with a hot breakfast.)

    ..  admonition:: GM Note

        If the party has no leads, Butcher will suggest staking out the *The Library* or *The Oaks* to see who comes and who goes. It's late, and characters can switch watches with Butcher so everyone will get a little sleep.

        -   There will be one or two furtive runners to and from *The Oaks*.

        -   Anyone watching *The Library* will see that Butcher's intelligence gathering isn't perfect.
            This place is quiet all night.

3.  In the morning of 16 Summer, follow up on any leads.
    They may elect to check out  :ref:`location.eagles.library` or :ref:`location.eagles.theoaks`.

The Library Bookstore
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The Librarian is one of the two possible contacts from the *Fox's Tail* Tavern (see :ref:`chapter.foxs_tail`.)  *The Library* Bookstore is a privately-run affair, not a public library.

*The Library* is close the center of the city, in a nice neighborhood.
It typically has a few low-level clerics and other scholars consulting with the Librarian.
Mages also deal in books here, but because of the way the Temple of Cyþan tends to horde books, the mages are more discreet.
The Library is set up a bit like a mini Temple of Cyþan to try and cater to mages.

See :ref:`location.eagles.library` for details of this location, and the people there.

The Oaks Riding Stables
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The boss at *The Oaks* Riding Stable is one of the two possible contacts from the *Fox's Tail* Tavern (see :ref:`chapter.foxs_tail`.)

The stables are outside the walls, right as you leave town. The place looks like it's about to fall over. The stable-hands look like professional thugs.

See :ref:`location.eagles.theoaks` for details of this location, and the people there.

Consequences
------------

There are a number of additional events later in the day.

16 Summer, late in the forenoon watch
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Squire Neck and Squire Starter are publicly berated by Sir Warrant for fighting; guests and hosts are expected to behave courteously.

It's a dry, formal statement, read by Sir Warrant with an apology to all present for this tedious necessity.

Butcher is outside the gate, watching folks coming and going.
If he makes a successful investigation roll (difficulty Difficult) he may see a messenger bumping into Steward.
Steward is milling around in the crowd waiting to hear what's going on in the knight's castle today.

..  admonition:: GM Note

    This isn't going to go well for Squire Neck
    back in the City of White Water.
    Somehow, he did make a mess of things.
    Sir Bond is unlikely to trust him with anything more than groom duties after this.

16 Summer, Noon
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Sir Bond leaves City of Eagles to return to White Water.
He's made it clear he is afraid there might be attempts on his life.

He, however, does not move in with any particular haste or secrecy down the South East road.
Most of his guards have already left to make sure there's room at the tavern a few km outside the City of Eagles.
His actions reveal confidence that doesn't align with his words about fearing assassination.

Someone in the party needs to make a difficulty Difficult *streetwise* Acumen roll.

..  admonition:: GM Note

    This roll is important, and the result of this will *also* influence events in :ref:`chapter.the_exchange`.

-   **Success**.
    They'll see Butcher staring at someone who looks like one of the knight's household servants. Butcher then suddenly walk over to a vendor, buy a small loaf of bread, and start eating it.
    Partway through his loaf of bread, Butcher will point out someone.
    Make a difficulty Moderate *streetwise* Acumen roll to note that Butcher had been keeping an eye on a merchant lingering at the edge of the crowd of onlookers; the merchant is now pushing his way past a vendor to head down a side street toward the north gate.

    ..  admonition:: GM Note

        The merchant is named 'Steward'.

        He'll head to *The Oaks*. If no one's there, he'll leave a note. He has good skills and will use *Hiding* to avoid detection as well as to conceal the note.
        The party's attempt to see him or find the note will be opposed rules with Heroic difficulty.

        If the bandits are still there, he'll talk with the stable boss. Anyone who was following him will be followed and attacked. This is not a good place to be.
        This requires an Acumen roll with a difficulty of Easy to note the danger.

        ..  include:: ../characters/ch_4_jackal_spymaster.txt


-   **Failure**.
    It's noon, time for some lunch, nothing abnormal.

..  admonition:: GM Note

    It's a small crowd with the curious, the beggars and pickpockets, and the food vendors. Not the kind of thing where a player would clearly want to invest a character point (or fate point) for a seemingly arbitrary perception check.

    However, the mere fact of asking players to make perception rolls often suggests there may be more at stake than simply thwarting a pickpocket.

One of Sir Warrant's servants will locate you in the crowd.
(Make a difficulty Moderately Difficult to note them pushing toward you.)

The servant scans the crowd while whispering to you,
"That Oven from White Water thinks she worked out a deal with Squire Starter. She hasn't slept all night. You see, she can barely sit her horse.

"Sir Bond argued with Oven on and off while she and Starter crept around the castle whispering and passing letters to each other.
We heard Sir Bond claim our Sir Warrant could have easily bribed or coerced agents to do exactly this.
Tell Butcher that Squire Angler is worried sick."

16th of Summer, afternoon and evening
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Angler holds court in a nice tavern, The :ref:`location.eagles.thirstycrow`, not far from the knight's castle.
He has to listen to grifters, liars, and confidence men with no real information, but an appetite for money.

The party needs to remain reasonably sober in case trouble breaks out.
The party's rooms are paid for.

The party needs to be at the knight's castle by the middle of Morning Watch (6 AM).
