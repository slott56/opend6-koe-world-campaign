..  _`chapter.white_water`:

Finish the Deal
==============================

:Location: :ref:`location.whitewater`, including :ref:`location.whitewater.candler` and :ref:`location.whitewater.haelan`, among possibly others.

:Date: 19th of Summer

:Time: Late Afternoon

..  structure: context complication crisis climax (i.e., battle) consequences.

Context
--------

The party arrives in the bustling City of White Water.
While the plan was to arrive with Squire Starter and the swords, there were complications along the way.
Because of the Cult of the Jackal, the party may have neither swords nor Squire Starter.

Complication
-------------

The party is on their own in a strange city, foundering in water over their head.

If the party has lost the swords (and Squire Starter) no one will believe the survivors.
They can't be from City of Eagles; they aren't members of Sir Warrant's family.
More importantly, Goblins are a rumor, not a credible threat.
The difficulty rolls required to make someone understand the party's mission start at Difficult.

..  admonition:: GM Note

    In the unlikely event that Squire Starter was **not** captured by the Goblins, he's generally useless here.
    As a Squire (of Sir Warrant) he *could* be an entry to the circles of power.
    Instead, he'll flop back and forth between aloof and confused.
    He'll start fights with anyone who disputes his account.

    If someone still has a token from :ref:`chapter.rod_is_lost`, this token will permit spy mages in White Water to create even greater levels of confusion.

The clock is ticking. The party needs to get back on the road to see where the goblins took Squire Starter. Three days (or two if they abused their horses) are past, and they still have three days (or two) to go.

Crisis
------

The first guards the party meets will treat any story about needing to find Sir Bond with callous indifference.

"Everyone wants to find Sir Bond," one guard says.

"If you don't know where he is, what real business do you have?" adds another.

"Move along," a third says.

Their sergeant, however, is interested in your claims.
He wants to hear your story again to be sure he's got it right.

(Difficulty moderate.)
He's got an unusual interest in your story.

He will direct the party to Priestess Bargeman at a Shrine to Hælan.
The Priestess can help take care of strays like you.
The route is easy: West Road down to "The Ladder", then back out the North Road.
Take first high street facing East.

..  admonition:: GM Note

    Followers of Witan help out strays and the lost.
    That's not a feature of the temple of Hælan.
    This is an difficulty Easy intelligence check for a cleric of any temple; difficulty Moderate check for non-clerics.

    A difficulty Easy check shows he's not misleading you, he really does believe what he's saying about the priestess.

Climax
------

There are two locations of interest:

-   :ref:`location.whitewater.haelan`
    In town, above the falls, is a shrine to -- something -- it might be a sculpture of a turtle or a pile of clay on the top of a plinth.
    It's called a shrine to Hælan.

    ..  admonition:: GM Note

        This is odd because there's a prominent temple a few streets away. Why have a shrine?
        This is an difficulty Easy intelligence check for a cleric of Hælan; difficulty Moderate check for non-clerics.

        Followers of Hælan won't recognize this icon.
        This is an difficulty Easy intelligence check for a cleric of Hælan; difficulty Moderate check for non-clerics.

-   :ref:`location.whitewater.candler`.
    A little south of town, below the falls, is a fat rendering plant.
    Candler makes candles and soap there, and sells them from a shop in town.
    He will **not** take anyone to see Sir Bond.

The party -- being from Hillshire -- is visible.
Squire Neck will find them sometime in the Evening Watch.
He's in a world of hurt for losing the Lightning Rod, and turning the swords over to him may not be the best idea.

Squire Neck has one thing on his mind: Retribution.
He can decompose this into three short-term goals: recover the Rod, recover the Swords, recover Squire Starter.

He wants to retreat to the Temple of Hildeþrymm, near The Ladder.
(Difficulty Easy to see he's sincere.)

This is neutral territory, the party can be confident nothing will happen there.
You can spend the night here, in the barracks, with the priests.

Tomorrow, you'll need to pick up the trail.

He will break the news to Sir Bond that things have gone wrong on the road and he's taking a force of men up to the border where he can gather details.
Since it will take him the rest of the next day to get some men organized, he's likely to be two days behind you.
Further, he can't really cross the border, so he's only helpful if someone like Squire Starter is around to legitimize his armed incursion.

Consequence
-----------

These encounters suggest White Water **is** behind the Bandit Army plaguing trade and City of Eagles.
Is Sir Bond aware of it, or is he clueless?

..  admonition:: GM Note

    The Goblin story is viewed with suspicion.
    Experts agree that goblins are rare and require a powerful mage with a huge fixed forge to summon them.
    Other experts do not agree.
    Sir Bond, however, doesn't listen to the other experts.
    Since he thinks goblins are rare, he puts his squires in an awkward position of knowing there are goblins, and having to repeat the knight's nonsense that they're vanishingly rare.

    Candler was known to Squire Neck, Oven, and Sir Bond.
    The scope of his activities was a mystery, but he seemed to bribe well.

    Sir Bond had a suspicion that someone like Priestess Bargeman was operating in White Water.
    He suspects someone else in town, and thinks Priestess Bargeman was playing the party: "the river rats do that to everyone, it's in their nature" is his standard line.

    Squire Neck doesn't know Bargeman's role for certain.
    He can't believe someone doesn't have a simple loyalty to one side or the other.
    He's sure that being a double agent is too much emotional stress.

    Oven is alarmed that her father puts up with this.
    She knows they need to watch both of these actors and prevent their meddling.
    She doesn't know how to make the "watch these actors" happen, though.

There are several alternative paths forward:

1.  Sign on as highwaymen at the *Wild Mare* tavern.
    The party can then infiltrate the bandit army, locate Squire Starter, and find out what happened to the **Lightning Rod**, and the swords.
    Squire Neck will tolerate this, but has a better idea.
    The bandit army are bloodthirsty thugs, and you could get hurt when you're force to attack a caravan.

    :next: :ref:`chapter.find_squire_starter` while part of the Bandit Army. The party will be brought into the gatehouse for orientation, and then assigned bunks in the New Keep.

2.  Sign on as pilgrims at the West Gate.
    The party can then infiltrate the Cult of the Jackal, locate Squire Starter, and find out what happened to the **Lightning Rod**, and the swords.
    Squire Neck will tolerate this, also, but has a better idea.
    We know so little about the Cult, infiltration could leak more details of our plans.

    :next: :ref:`chapter.find_squire_starter` while part of the Cult of the Jackal. The party will be staying in the East Lodge for a few days.

3.  Follow the goblin's trail.
    Go back down the road to where the abduction happened, track the goblins. Find Squire Starter or their hide-out or some explanation for how goblins entered our realm.
    Officially, Sir Bond doesn't like giving credence to the presence of goblins.
    However, following the trail of the abductors does make sense.

    :next: :ref:`chapter.find_squire_starter` via one of the two Goblin Caves.

4.  Go back to City of Eagles.
    This means Squire Neck and and the experts here in White Water can deal with it.
    This is touchy because of the armed incursion into Hillshire that would be required.

    ..  admonition:: GM Note

        This leads to war.

    To leverage the resulting chaos, the party needs to seize control of the Bandit Army.

    :next: :ref:`chapter.find_squire_starter`.
