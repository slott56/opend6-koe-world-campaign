Find Squire Starter
==============================

:Location: Somewhere in Hillshire, :ref:`location.keep`.

:Date: Late summer (as early as the 19th, more likely the 21st)

:Time: Late Afternoon

..  structure: context complication crisis climax (i.e., battle) consequences.

..  admonition:: GM Note

    City of Eagles will take the Jackal threat seriously.
    There will be no mercy and no resupply of pilgrims or highwaymen through Eagles.
    *The Oaks* will be auctioned to someone else.
    The *Fox's Tail* Tavern will be subject to periodic "surveillance" by the city guard.
    This means different squires under Sir Warrant will lead parties of guards to confront the thugs that collect here.

    City of White Water will shut down Candler's network of contacts. Without implicit trust from the powers of White Water, Bargeman will be forced to flee downriver to the City of Grace.

    These two actions mean the Temple of the Jackal will be isolated and will not get any resupply.
    This will give the party considerable flexibility in their operations, free from outside interference.
    This won't obvious, and the party should be encouraged to feel a sense of urgency.

Context
-------

The characters can't waste any more time, they've got find Squire Starter, the **Lighting Rod**, and Sir Warrant's swords.

(Or, possibly, they kept the swords and skipped :ref:`chapter.white_water`.)

There are a number of paths that can converge here:

1.  The party is a band of Highwaymen recruits from Candler, reporting to the Temple with a bandit escort.
    (See :ref:`chapter.white_water`, and :ref:`location.whitewater.wildmare`.)
    Once inside the Temple, the party will have one night to explore while they are being equipped and briefed.
    They will be in barracks in the New Keep building.
    Security is pretty tight. Most of the bandits don't care about the Jackal; they're in it for the head bashing, sweetened by any money they collect from merchants.

2.  The part is a band of Pilgrim recruits from Bargeman, reporting to the Temple with a pilgrim escort.
    (See :ref:`chapter.white_water`, and :ref:`location.whitewater.west_gate`.)
    One inside Xorn's Temple, the party will have 3 nights to explore.
    They will be housed in the lodge outside the wall.
    Goblins roam around at night.
    Security is less tight. Other pilgrims will be wary of the party.

3.  Following the goblin's trail from the abduction site. There are two variations:

    -   Keeping the swords, never going to White Water, and pursuing the goblins right after the abduction.
        (See :ref:`chapter.the_exchange`, :ref:`location.hillshire.cliff`.)

    -   Returning from White Water to the cliffs, and  following the goblins' path to the caves.
        In this case, Squire Neck can offer the possibility armed support.
        They will be following two days behind you, but they'll have to stop at the border.
        (See :ref:`chapter.white_water`.)

4.  Captured by the Bandit army and dragged to the Temple.
    This is -- perhaps -- the worst case, since security is pretty tight.
    (See :ref:`chapter.the_exchange`, :ref:`location.hillshire.cliff`.)

5.  Captured by Goblin raiders and dragged to a cave.
    Goblin cave security is lax, rushing the guards is possible.
    (See :ref:`chapter.the_exchange`, :ref:`location.hillshire.cliff`.)

Complications
-------------

If the party tries to go back to White Water or City of Eagles for support, there are two risks:

-   The `Bandit Army`_ patrolling the roads by day.

-   The `Goblin Raiders`_ patrolling the hills by night.

The risk of capture varies with the state of the forces in the Temple.

Bandit Army
~~~~~~~~~~~

The army patrols by day most days.
See :ref:`location.keep.schedules` for the daily schedule. Some days the patrols are light. Other days, the patrols are more serious.

In the event the party raises havoc inside the Temple -- obvious stealing of artifacts, breaking or destroying things, killing officers, etc. -- then the bandits will be broken into two forces: external and internal.

The external force will patrol the road, making it difficult to get to either town for support.

The internal force will start room-by-room searches.
See `crisis`_ below.

..  admonition:: GM Note

    There are about 48 bandits in :ref:`location.keep.new_keep.level_2`.
    Evading capture during the day has a base difficulty Moderate 15.
    Every character must make this roll to avoid capture.

    Killing or injuring 10% (initially 5 or more) of the bandits decimates the force. They become more timid, the difficulty of evading capture drops by 2.
    More damage to bandits will lead to freedom of movement during the day.

    After killing 10 bandits, the difficulty of evading capture drops from difficulty of Moderate to Easy.
    There will be morale checks among the survivors, and any further attrition counts as a reduction in the force as devastating as a death.

    Also, if a general alarm was raised, the external force is reduced by 1/2, and the difficulty to evade capture drops by 2.


Goblin Raiders
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The goblins are on the surface outside only at night.
They enter and exit from their caves after dark.
See :ref:`location.keep.caves.east` and :ref:`location.keep.caves.south`.

Each cave has separate hordes with separate patrol areas.
The East Cave horde covers the route to the City of White Water.
The South Cave horde covers the route to the City of Eagles.

..  admonition:: GM Note

    There is very lax security inside the goblin caves.
    If captured by goblins, the party has more than one chance to rush their captors and -- perhaps -- fight their way free.

    There are about 48 goblins in each horde.
    Evading capture at night has a base difficulty Difficult 18.
    Every character must make this roll to avoid capture.

    Killing 10% (initially 5 or more) of a goblin cave's horde decimates that horde. The horde becomes more timid; the difficulty of evading capture drops by 2 each wave of decimation.

    After killing 10 bandits, the difficulty of evading capture drops from difficulty of Moderate to Easy.
    More damage to goblins will lead to freedom of movement under cover of darkness.

Crisis
------

The objective is to find three things:

-   Squire Starter.

-   The **Lightning Rod**.

-   The swords (if they were also lost with Squire Starter.)

..  admonition:: GM Note

    See :ref:`location.keep.central_tower` for the bulk of the loot.

There are guards. And officers. And priests. And Xorn.

There is little occasion for a general alarm in the event of any isolated fighting with individual guards or small groups.
There is a constant stream of hostage torturing and human sacrifice.
Fighting, death and bloodshed are the norm.
If the party is careful, a trail of bodies and bloodshed might be overlooked.

With divination (difficulty of Very Difficult) the party may be able to avoid room-by-room searching.

With investigation skills (difficulty of Difficult) the party may conclude the Central Tower is the most likely place for Squire Starter and the **Lightning Rod**.

..  admonition:: GM Note

    A general alarm requires something like obvious stealing of artifacts, breaking or destroying things, killing officers, breaking down doors, lighting fires, etc.

Climax
------

Xorn (and whomever is handy among his forces) will try to protect his hostage and his prize.

The low-level guards are too afraid to enter the upper levels of the central tower.

Only the senior-most priests will enter the upper levels of the Central Tower.

..  admonition:: GM Note

    When damage reaches the Severely Wounded level, or Xorn is somehow cornered, he will flee in secret through the deep tunnels of the Underkeep to a secret cave near the hot spring.

    See :ref:`location.cave`, the setting of :ref:`chapter.flush_the_jackal`.

Consequence
------------

Unless the General of the Bandit Army is killed, the army will continue to defend the Temple.
This defense will make it very difficult to escape with Squire Starter and the **Lightning Rod**.
If the General is killed, 50% of the surviving Bandit army will flee into the hills.

The remaining bandits, are still subject to a morale check each time 10% or more are killed.

With investigation or tracking skills (difficulty of Moderate) the party may find the path Xorn took to the Underkeep. With hiding and stealth skills (difficulty of Easy to Moderate) the party may escape without running into the Bandit Army.

The Jackal Cult, on the other hand, will collapse into confused internal arguments and squabbling.
Various priests will claim to be in charge, and will be utterly ineffective at defending the Temple.

There are several choices for the party:

-   Return to City of Eagles.
    Now the knights are back on equal footing with an exchange to artifacts.
    They can clear out what remains of the Jackal cult, and Sir Warrant can try to improve the position of City of Eagles.

    This is worth a Fate Point.

    The knights can assemble a force to neutralize the temple, sending in Squire Neck and Squire Starter.

    Or, the knights can leave the cleanup operation to the party.
    The party can continue to explore the Temple, locating the last holdouts, finding the books and the records and learning where Xorn may be hiding.

-   Return to City of White Water.
    Now Sir Bond has all the artifacts, and both children.
    He is in a position to dictate terms to Sir Warrant, something the party should have prevented.

    The party won't earn a Fate Point if they make this choice.

    Sir Warrant will told to assemble a force to neutralize the temple.
    The party can volunteer for this cleanup operation.
    This will allow the party to explore the Temple, locating the last holdouts, finding the books and the records and learning where Xorn may be hiding.

-   Try to take control of the Temple and assume power.
    With enough Charisma, this may be possible. It requires coercing **all** of the priests and the officers of the Bandit Army.

    This is a different kind of outcome, and is a way to earn a Fate Point.

In all cases, there remains the problem of tracking down Xorn.
