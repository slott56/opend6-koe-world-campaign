####################################
  Temple of the Cult of the Jackal
####################################

Concealed in a wide, flat hollow, high in the hills, lies this obscure structure.
It's neither large, nor imposing.
It's not designed to intimidate by hulking on the edge of a steep hill, and providing a commanding view of a valley.
It's nestled deep in the hills, designed to remain as a secure, hidden retreat, surrounded by higher peaks.

..  note:: Jackals are not part of this continent's wildlife.

    Somewhere on the continent to the North, there's a place with a Jackal-headed god, who looks like the Egyptian Anubis.

    People who are casually aware of the cult often substitute "wolf" for "jackal" because that's an animal they know.
    Traders and travelers in the western continent may use "coyote", because it makes them sound more worldly.

    The true followers never make a mistake. To them, the term "jackal" becomes a kind of passcode; something outsiders wouldn't know.

The temple's valley is a bit less than a kilometer long in the E-W direction.
A number of little streams in rocky channels converge in a pond, and drop over a fall to a lower, narrower valley.
In the N-S direction, it's a bit less than a half-kilometer wide.

The location of the Temple is a closely-guarded secret.
Pilgrims need directions, and must contact one of the spies in Eagles to get directions and the password.

The most effective entry is to get captured by the bandit army and dragged up here.


General
=======

..  figure::  ../figures/JackalKeep.png

    Temple Grounds

    Scale: Hex = 2m (6').

The structure is just under 140 m along the north-facing wall.
The western wall is 60 m long.
The eastern wall is 40 m long.
The southern wall has two turns in it: walking the top is 162 m.
The total circuit, marched by the guards is a hair over 400m.

..  admonition:: GM Note

    Given a typical character's Move value of 10m/r, it's possible for an officer to hustle around the wall in about four minutes.
    Since there's no reason to hurry, a typical circuit takes about 12-16 minutes, with long pauses at each tower to stare into the distance.

    As a general guideline -- if a character makes a simple Acumen-related observation skill roll --
    use 4D minutes as the time for a circuit by a guard.

There are a few obvious structures that comprise the larger temple structure.

-   **Wall**.
    The compound wall is crenelated, only 8-10m high, with five small towers in the corners.
    The visible force is about 30 men. The towers are 10m high.

    The rumor about other-worldly goblins patrolling the forest around the keep after nightfall is true.

-   **Gate House**.
    The Gate House is 8-9m high, with a flat roof, and crenelation, allowing armed archers to shoot down into the road.
    It has a double door, which open during the day.
    The front has archer slits that house some guards with cross-bows, plus the keeper of the gate.
    Inside the gatehouse are spiral stairs leading to the roof.

    A concealed door (difficulty Easy 8, to discover because it's heavily used) reveals stairs  descending into a small basement.

    Pilgrims are admitted with a little discussion beyond some secret phrases or gestures.

    Hirelings and merchants with supplies must present paperwork to be granted access to the temple.

-   **The New Keep**.
    The New Keep is the cult's nerve center.
    It houses the human guards, as well as his planning staff and intelligence gatherers.
    It has the main dungeons, as well as some magical equipment for spying out the doings in various cities.

-   **The Central Tower**.
    The Central Tower is the Temple of the Jackal.
    Religious ceremonies and magical procedures are performed here.
    The central nave is open for five stories, and occasionally has birds and what-not flying around.

    The forest is ancient, and does have trees taller than this tower.
    From the cleared road, and the surrounding hills, the tower is prominent because it erupts out of a large clearing.

-   **The Old Keep**.
    The Old Keep is an ancient building with old-style haphazard stone work.
    It has been added-to and extended over the centuries.

    While it once was the main building, it is now the private residence of Xorn and his main henchmen.

-   **Barn and Stables**.
    Up against the north wall are two small wooden structures.
    They have the usual features of barns and stables: horses, manure, farming tools, hay.

    Outside the barn is a 30m × 4m garden plot with some herbs and vegetables.

-   **The Lodges**.
    There are two outside lodges for Pilgrims: called the East Lodge and the West Lodge.
    These are 14m by 20m buildings.
    They're two stories above ground, with exposed foundations suggesting a basement.
    (They're not like common rural farmhouses built over the animal pen.)

Less obvious are two caves.
The East Cave and South Cave are snuggled in deep, dry ravines, hidden by piles of boulders.
With some careful searching (difficulty Moderate 14) the caves can be found.
The ravines in which the caves are concealed are suspiciously dry; the water flows down a gulley not far away.
The caves are inhabited by a horde of otherworldy goblins.

It's not clear precisely how a horde of goblins were pulled into this realm, or for what purpose.
It's not even clear when they might have arrived; they may be marooned here.

Characters
==========

There are three distinct forces within the Temple.

-   The Cult of the Jackal. This is a force composed of clerics, novices, and pilgrims. This group is the base for the externally focused intelligence operation in City of Eagles and City of White Water.

-   The Bandit Army with mages and various thugs and criminals.

-   There is **also** a horde of Goblins that sometimes infests the temple valley.

The Cult of the Jackal is the religious arm.

----

See :ref:`character.xorn` in the :ref:`character` chapter.

Residence: `Central Tower Fourth Level`_ and `Central Tower Fifth Level`_.

Cult of the Jackal
------------------

The organization has two divisions, internal and external.

-   **Internal Security**.
    These priests are in charge of scrying and torture.
    There are devices in the top of the New Keep that are used to discern caravan movements along the road, and direct the bandit army to intercept them.

    The head of this organization the Senior Priest -- Internal (budget 22D + 13D).

    Office: `New Keep Ground Level`_, Room 9. More commonly, however, they're found in the `New Keep Fourth Level`_, rooms 1 and 2.

    Residence: Old Keep, Level 3, Room 2.

    There are direct reports to the Senior Priest of Internal Security: a priest and a magician.

    -   High Priest -- Torturer

        Office: `Old Keep Second Level`_, Room 2.

        Residence: `Old Keep Third Level`_, Room 5.

    -   High Mage -- Scrying

        Office: `Old Keep Second Level`_, Room 2

        Residence: `Old Keep Third Level`_, Room 6

-   **External Intelligence**.
    These priests are in charge of communications, spies and disinformation.
    This organization is in contact with agents in City of Eagles and City of White Water.

    The head of this organization is a Senior Priest External (budget 18D + 10D).

    Office: `New Keep Ground Level`_, Room 7.

    Residence: `Old Keep Third Level`_, Room 4.

    The organization has two High Priests within the temple, writing and reading correspondence,
    and handling external operations, including finance and payroll for hirelings.
    There are two more High Priests operating in the field.

    -   Level 2-3 High Priest -- Communications

        Office: `New Keep Ground Level`_, Room 7.

        Residence: `Old Keep Third Level`_, Room 7.
        Hot-bunking with Operations.
        Really, quite awkward.

    -   Level 2-3 High Priest -- Operations

        Office: `New Keep Ground Level`_, Room 7.

        Residence: `Old Keep Third Level`_, Room 7.
        Hot-bunking with Communications.
        Not happy with this.

    -   Level 2-3 High Priest.

        Deployed in City of Eagles.
        His cover is to represent some vague 'investors' from Southlands looking for trade opportunities: call him 'Steward'.

        Two henchmen:

        - Boss at *The Oaks* riding Stable.

        - Bartender from *The Fox's Tail*.

        See :ref:`chapter.the_exchange` for details on the spymaster in City of Eagles.


    -   Level 2-3 High Priest.

        Deployed in City of White Water at a fat rendering plant out of town.  Makes and sells candles from a shop in town.

        See :ref:`chapter.white_water` for details on the spymaster in City of White Water.

Bandit Army
------------

The Bandit army's base is the Jackal's Temple.
However, their field of operations is throughout the eastern edge of Hillshire and the western edge of Southlands.
See :ref:`location.keep`.

The bandit army is -- well -- bandits.
There is little coordination among the forces.
They use looted armor and weapons.
They steal ideas from the goblins that live in the caves near the temple.

It is bloody chaos.

The organization is as follows:

-   General

    During the day, found in the field, raiding.

    Residence: New Keep, Level 2, rooms 10, 12, 14.

Wall and Corner Towers
======================

The wall is 8m high for the most part. The towers are 10m.

The towers are numbered clockwise around the compound.

..  csv-table::
    :header-rows: 1

    Tower,Hex Coordinate,Description
    1,68.4,"NE Corner"
    2,49.24,"SE Corner, W of gate house"
    3,30.30,"S Wall, W of lodge"
    4,2.30,"SW Corner"
    5,2.4,"NW Corner"

They're all largely empty. Each has a weapons rack with 1D shabby swords, 2D worn spears, 1D crossbows, and a shallow crate of 5D+10 bolts.

Gate House
==========

The road leads up to the gate house. To it's west, there are two lodges, outside the wall, and seemingly undefended.

The gate is flanked by walls with a few arrow slits to allow archers to shoot down on someone approaching the gate.

The outermost door -- made of heavy timbers, iron straps, and massive studs -- appears to never close.
There are weeds growing around it.

The inner portcullis is raised and lowered every day.

The Underkeep
=============

Before the Gatehouse and Walls (which are old) there was the Old Keep.
Before the Old Keep, there was something else here, which was buried and forgotten, rebuilt, torn down, buried and forgotten a second time.

There are two distinct parts to the Underkeep, the Old portion is mostly under the the current locations of the Old Keep and Gate House.
The New portion is mostly under the Central Tower.
The terms old and new are relative to each other, of course; all of the surface construction is newer than the new portion of the Underkeep.

Careful study (difficulty Moderate 15, with access to the Old portion of the underkeep) can reveal the Old Keep was built from rubble of an even older construction that once stood here.
Further, the part underlying the Old Keep is relatively small, with only 5 distinct rooms.

To the west, under the Central Tower is a distinct Underkeep.
This is newer, and far more confusing.

There's a connection between them: a hidden door between 40.2 and 40.3.
The design of the catch that holds the door closed is awkwardly high, and requires a great deal of leverage to operate.
The difficulty Moderate 14 to find the hidden recess.
Operating the catch is a difficulty Difficult 16. Taking time to study it, and improvising some tools will reduce this to a more a 12.

South Cave
============

About 80m from the tower in the Southwest corner of the keep, on the sloping side of a boulder-strewn hillside,
there's the carefully hidden South Cave.

The spoor of raiding Goblins is often cleaned away; it's difficulty Moderate 15 to notice the traces.
