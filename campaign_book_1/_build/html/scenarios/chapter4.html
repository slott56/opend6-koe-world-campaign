4. The Exchange¶

Location:

The road to White Water, including Gulley Near City of Eagles and Cliff Near the Border.

Date:

17 Summer

Time:

2 Hours into Morning Watch (6 AM)

Context¶

Sir Bond’s Lightning Rod was not in any of his squires’ or men-at-arms’ gear. His grooms turned the stables upside down. There’s one dead thug, and Butcher’s pretty sure he’s one of the gang that circulates around The Fox’s Tail and The Oaks stable.

Sir Warrant has to make a good faith show that he’s trustworthy. He’ll offer his two very powerful swords, IceKnife and GoblinDeath, with his son, Squire Starter, as the envoy.

Sir Warrant doesn’t want to make a full military show out of this. He needs to work closely with Squire Angler to find out who stole the Lightning Rod, and why. Butcher put some men in place to watch the roads, but it may be too late. Even though the city gates were closed, gates are defenses to keep armies out. It’s easy for a few people to slip out a postern, or drop a bundle over the wall to a waiting accomplice.

Sir Warrant is pacing in the courtyard of his small castle. His commission to Squire Starter (and the party) is to get these two swords to Sir Bond as soon as possible, while he and the other squires find the Lightning Rod.

Squire Starter’s job is to placate White Water. He’s got money and letters to set up a rapid communications channel with riders and horses at each royal tavern, so they can make the trip in less than a day.

If Squire Starter never arrives with the swords, war seems unavoidable.

If the son somehow suffers an epic failure at keeping Sir Bond informated, Sir Bond will probably imprison him as a hostage and threaten war. The party will need to return with as much advance notice as they can provide.

It’s important for Sir Warrant to have reliable folks that aren’t part of the formal, liveried hierarchy of squires and men-at-arms. Some things need to be done discretely, out of sight of the earls or the king.

Complication¶

Squire Starter can be annoying. He insists on doing everything first; he is the squire in charge of the party. Against everyone’s better judgement, he will ride at the lead of the party and try to make all arrangements with the inns along the way. Against everyone’s better judgement, he will have Goblin Death strapped to his saddlebags, and Ice Knife in a scabbard on his saddle. (This will be his undoing.)

The distance to White Water is 80km, about 5 days of travel on foot. There are royal taverns every 16 km or so, spaced to cover a good day’s walking. On horseback, one tavern is a mid-day stop and the next is the nightly stop. Riding, the party can arrive at noon the 19th; walking they would arrive at dusk the 21st.

(A horse can be pushed to cover 48 km, combining skipping two taverns and staying in the third. This is potentially hazardous to the horse, especially in Hillshire.)

The negotiations, marriage offers, dowries, and now these swords have one purpose: to make a more concerted effort at stopping bandits along this road. This will yield clear benefits for City of Eagles, and Hillshire in general. Stealing the Lightning Rod and wrecking the negotiations is the work of bandits who have gotten too powerful and want to prevent any useful alliances.

The swords:

Ice Knife: Agility 1D, Coordination 3D, Physique 1D, Intellect 1D, Acumen 3D, Charisma 2D, persuasion +1. Disadvantages: Advantage Flaw (R3), wants to rampage and kill everything., Special Abilities: Natural Hand-to-Hand Weapon (R2D+1), +1D damage to creatures vulnerable to cold, Move: 10, Strength Damage: 1D, Fate Points: 1, Character Points: 5, Body Points: 27.

Goblin Death: Agility 1D, Coordination 3D, Physique 1D, Intellect 1D, Acumen 3D, Charisma 2D. Disadvantages: Advantage Flaw (R3), insists on killing the goblins it detects., Special Abilities: Natural Hand-to-Hand Weapon (R2D+1), ; Armor-Defeating Attack (R2), Reduces armor by -2; Extra Sense (R2), Detects goblins and other creatures from the same realm, Move: 10, Strength Damage: 1D, Fate Points: 1, Character Points: 5, Body Points: 24.

Crisis¶

The party will be followed by the spy leader from the Temple of the Jackal, plus a hired thug leader, and five thugs. The thugs will be watching the bridge to see when the party leaves town. They will leave an hour later to catch up along the way.

Acting friendly, Steward’s party will seek to pass, offering to reserve some rooms in the next inn. In 16 Summer, Noon, someone in the party made a important difficulty Difficult streetwise Acumen roll. If the party made this roll successfully, they’ll recognize Steward as the merchant watching Sir Bond depart. If the party failed the roll, and didn’t see who Butcher was following, this is merely a bunch of scary-looking guys riding to a village down the road.

The Cult of the Jackal has scrying devices, and can follow Squire Starter’s progress. From the token, they’ve managed to establish a fitful link with him. Spy-master Steward is the least of the party’s problems.

Climax¶

There are two separate ambushes:

17th Summer, late in the afternoon¶

In a gully a few kilometers before the first of the Royal Inns, Steward and his bandits will attack.

See Gulley Near City of Eagles for details of this location, Steward, and the group of hired thugs he’s bringing with him.

For the survivors, the tavern will be peaceful and there will be food, beds, and medical supplies (in case the party needs them).

Folks are curious about White Water going up to Eagles, then hurrying back the next day. It can help if the party discloses nothing. In the event someone fails to keep secrets, this can alter the events in Finish the Deal.

18th Summer, forenoon¶

The border with Southlands is about 40 km away from Eagles; the party expects to start its descent from Hillshire late on the second morning.

Early in the day, the road passes between a cliff (10m above) and a narrow river (30m or more below). The road is reasonably wide (6-7m), but twists around the river’s course. (The slope down down to the river is too steep for horses.)

See Cliff Near the Border for details of this location, the Goblin assault troops, and the supporting squad from the Bandit Army.

Consequences¶

The party has a choice to make:

  • Chase the goblins.

  • Deliver the swords.

  • Split the party. (This seems like certain death.)

A roll with difficulty Difficulty using Scholar Intellect will allow a character to recall a few important things that can help make this decision:

  1. Goblins are not from this realm; they’re from an ælvish realm of mountains and forests.

  2. Goblins don’t like our bright sun. To be out during the day suggests some kind of strong compulsion or command.

  3. To avoid killing a victim also suggests they’re not going to eat him.

GM Note

If the party defeats the goblins entirely, they’re still faced with the same choices: go to Finish the Deal, or use a Tracking skill to figure out where the goblins came from: use Find Squire Starter.

Chase the Goblins¶

If the swords aren’t delivered, Sir Bond and Sir Warrant are in very awkward positions, and war is inevitable.

There is a strong possibility of skirmishes with the afterguard of the goblin assault force. The bandit army is roaming through the hills.

Anyone trying to pursue the goblins will find the two ambushes waiting for them

  • One-third of the goblin survivors. They will set an ambush. They will seek to subdue the party and imprison them in the caves.

    A difficulty Moderately Difficulty Tracking Acumen roll will reveal this, allowing the party to have the initiative.

  • A band of 2D highwaymen. They will seek to subdue the party and imprison them in the cells under the Central Tower.

    A difficulty Moderately Difficult Tracking Acumen roll will reveal this, allowing the party to have the initiative.

Next:

Find Squire Starter.

Deliver the swords¶

This will allow the party to return in about a week. It seems unlikely Squire Starter will have been skinned and eaten. The goblins made great pains to drag him away, not killing him or trying to slaughter the rest of the party.

(If the party punishes their horses, they can be back in four to five days.)

If the party departs promptly, any goblin pursuit will be at a safe distance with no fighting. The Bandit Army will not put in an appearance.

Next:

Finish the Deal.

Split the Party¶

This seems like certain death.

Anyone trying to pursue the goblins will find the two ambushes waiting for them

  • One-third of the goblin survivors. They will seek to subdue the party and imprison them in the caves.

  • A band of 2D highwaymen. They will seek to subdue the party and imprison them in the cells under the Central Tower.

Once captured, this fraction of the party will be waiting around for Find Squire Starter to locate them.

The rest of the party will make it to the next tavern. They will be able to move to Finish the Deal.
