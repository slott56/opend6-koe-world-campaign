2. The Dinner Rush¶

Location:

The Fox’s Tail Tavern. Details are in the Locations section.

Date:

15 Summer

Time:

Early Evening

Cast:

Drinkers, the Bartender, and some bands of hired thugs.

Context¶

Within the City of Eagles are some pretty rough neighborhoods. The south valley, down by the river is one of the worst. The poorest folk are spread along river, prone to being flooded out in spring.

The Fox’s Tail is in the roughest part of this neighborhood. The party can start there to find out why they were attacked. See Fox’s Tail Tavern.

The bar is – as expected – a dump. There is a cock fighting (or dog fighting pit) outside. The general attitude seems to be surly, in spite of a bunch of drunks singing badly. (The song generally offensive.)

GM Note

The party may not want to get involved with the hard cases in this tavern. The party has to choose between being at the knight’s castle on time, or hanging around The Fox’s Tail and getting into more trouble.

If they talk with the bartender, they must encounter the second batch of thugs. See the Consequences section.

If the party doesn’t talk with the bartender, then they can get away to reach the knight’s castle (and The Rod is Lost) on time.

Complication¶

The party can try to get some information from the bartender.

He won’t talk until the end of the Evening Watch rush, (around 7:00 PM). At this time, the party can only talk for a few minutes and still be at the knight’s castle by the start of the Dog Watch (around 8:00 PM).

GM Note

This chapter may be revisited after as part of The Rod is Lost. If the party waits, then there’s no need to wait until the end of the Evening Watch rush. The place is jumping, but the dinner crowd has moved on.

Crisis¶

If someone in the party has Interrogation or Charm skills, and the pary lingers in the tavern, they might be able to get some information from the bartender.

GM Note

If the characters blow it badly, the bartender will alert his crony at The Oaks Riding stable. After multiple failures for interrogation or charm skills. The bartender will clam up.

As soon as the party leaves, the bartender will send a servant to alert the thugs at The Oaks riding stable (see The Rod is Lost, and The Oaks Riding Stable. The thugs will clear out, abandoning the stable, rejoining the bandit army in the field.

Climax¶

Leaving the Fox’s Tail in order to be at the knight’s castle at the start of the Dog Watch (8:00 PM) leads to a potential problem. A second group of thugs is loitering in the streets. If you make a Streetwise skill roll (difficulty Difficult 16, they are concealed), you’ll recognize what is going on, otherwise, the things will have initiative when they make their surprise attack.

Consequences¶

If the party stays to talk with the bartender and fails to interrogate or persuade him, there will be adverse consequences:

  • They will be thrown out, and told to return in the morning.

  • They will get tangled up with the thugs, and be late trying to get to the knight’s castle. Squire Angler is angry and there are questions about where you were last night.

Next:

The Exchange.

If the party avoids the thugs and stays on the good side of the bartender, they will be on time for the reception.
