PC’s have to find him by making the right kind of PER rolls at the right time, or by having a good Interrogation skill roll.

		- 15 Summer, Evening.  Bad things happen.
			- White Water visits Eagles
				White Water is visiting City of the Eagles to attempt marriage negotiation again.  This is a major event, since the Baron of White Wate is paranoid of spies and assassination.  He can only force himself to travel if he has a heavily armed retinue.  He also spends months setting up a series of spy rings in City of the Eagles.  The Baron of Eagles has to tolerate this to prevent needless friction.  It requires monitoring numerous traders craftsmen throughout town.
White Water brought some dowery items, mostly valuable trinkets - furs, jewels, gold and such.  Secretly, however, he has also brought the powerful Lightning Rod.  White Water’s Guard Captain is personally responsible for the Rod.
			- 10PM, Son of Eagles found fighting with White Water’s Guard Captain
				That night, there was a reception in the Baronial Tower scheduled.  During the dancing after dinner, the Son of Eagles was found fighting with White Water’s Guard Captain in the stables.  The Captain is out of uniform and flees, badly beat up.
Eagle’s Son has a confused story about having a house guard tell him about a band of thieves in the stables.  He can’t remember which guard told him.  He claims he found the Captain of the Guard helping the thieves to make off with Lightning Rod.  The dead ruffian in the stable, however, was killed by the Captain’s bloody mace.
There’s no explanation for why the Captain was out of his uniform, or why he was in the stables at night and not at the reception.
Your friend, the Captain of the Guard of the City of Eagles, is convinced that some kind of outsider is causing these troubles.  Your lord, the Baron of the City of Eagles would never steal this, he wants a negotiated peace, including a succesful marriage.  White Waters might have done this to create an excuse for war, but if he did, he’d have turned in some lackey for punishment, not his own Guard Captain.
			- Rod is missing - Consequences
				The Lightning Rod was brought in secret, only a few knew it was there.  If the Baron of White Water makes any public accusations, everyone in White Water will label him a fool for losing such a powerful artifact.  His only choice is to complain about the paltry offer for marriage, storm off in a huff and declare war to get the artifact back.
Eagles must turn the countryside upside down to find the Rod or face open war from White Water.  This war had been simmering for years.  This is the excuse White Water needed.  For all the Baron of Eagles knows, this is all a fake - White Water might have taken the rod back just to start the fight.

Who confused the Baron’s son to attack the wrong man?  Most likely, he’s associated with “The Fox’s Tail”  If the characters have been there, but learned nothing, they might want to sneak back to avoid another attack.  They might want to contact the Anti-Xorn Bounty-Hunter for help with this.

However, this third band of ruffians will be lead by Xorn’s Spy from the riding stable and Xorn’s spy Henchman to beef up the attack.  If this happens, the stables will be abandoned, everything cleaned out.

			- The Plan
				The Rod has definitely left town.  Eagles realizes he must placate White Witer by exchanging hostages.  He will create a deal by exchanging his two very powerful swords, with his son as the envoy.  He expects the
You have Eagle’s two magical swords (IceKnife and GoblinDeath) to hand over to White Water until his Rod turns up.  You must escort the Baron's jerk son to the City of White Water to hand over the swords.  GoblinDeath is you lord's own sword, do not lose it! 
If the son is successful at placating White Water, he has learned a great deal, and may become Baron after all.
If the son fails, White Water will probably kill or improson him and start a war.  You will need to return with as much advance notice as you can provide.
If the son or the swords never arrive, war is also certain.  Do not turn aside for anything!
If you succeed, you are heroes, and will be well cared for, possibly knighted.  If you fail, you’ll be dead. White Waters will detain his son (and the swords) until the matter is resolved.
			- GM Notes
				The Son of the Baron of White Water is an annoying man.  And he insists on doing everything first.  Against everyone’s better judgement, he will ride (or walk) at the lead of the party and try to make all arrangements with the inns along the way.  This will be his undoing.
The Distance is 50 miles, about 50 hours of travel on foot (5 days).
Downhill walk from City of Eagles to City of White Waters.  Road is sometimes narrow and winding.
There are inns every 10 miles or so, a good day’s walking.  If you are on horseback, you can go about twice as fast, stopping at every other inn and arriving at noon the 18th, instead of dusk the 20th.

	- Recover the Rod
		- 20th (or 21st) Summer.  Find the Baron’s Son.
			- The Plan.
				The characters can’t waste any more time, they’ve got to get back to the cliff along the road and look for the Son of the Baron.  (Or, possibly, they kept the swords and skipped part E).
The Guard Captain of White Water can patch things up with his boss, if he is responsible for recovering the son of the Baron of the City of Eagles.  He has to make sure that the right people know the Son was grabbed by goblins; and the right people know he is orchestrating the rescue.
Your job is to find out about the goblins.  You are to then meet a troop of soldiers on the road by the cliff where the son was grabbed.  They’ll be arriving about 2 days after you do.
You might not want to cooperate with this Guard Captain.  It’s possible, he has designs of his own.  Your best bet might be to do the rescue yourself and get home as quickly as possible.
			- GM Notes
				They will have to enter Xorn’s keep to look for the Baron’s Son.  The keep is located in the hills south of both Eagles and White Water.  It is easy to locate, there was once a town there.
There are several ways in.  An obvious one is to go back to where the son was kidnapped and follow the goblin trail back to their cave.  Other is to join or follow the pilgrims who go there.  A third alternative is to travel with wealthy merchants and get waylaid by highwaymen.  Or, you could be recruited to join the highwaymen that Xorn uses to harass travelers.
Yet another choice is to go to a nearby village when the Goblin raiders come out at night.  This is more subtle, as no spy coordinates this activity.  This option will be proposed by the Guard Captain, since this is the kind of thing he hears about from the villages near Xorn’s keep.
			- Some Political Background
				The nobles are again on speaking terms.  Your envoy, plus some additional information from various spies, proves that some mischief is afoot.  White Water thinks it is the City of Eagles trying some complex ploy, trading him two swords for his Staff.  Eagles correctly sees that some outside influence is the cause of the problem.

			- Inside the Keep
				Once you are inside the keep, the encounters come hot and heavy and are based on the Xorn’s Keep details, below.  There is very little occaision for a “general alarm” since there is a constant stream of torturing and human sacrifice.  Fighting, death and bloodshed are the norm in this hell-hole.
		- GM Notes
			The players may try any number of plans.  Ideally, they will nose around for a while, watch the motions of the guards, the timing of the ceremonies, and realize that if they wait a couple of days, they can sneak around during a religious ceremony with near perfect impunity.  While there are a fair number of guards, there is no real organization, so each battle, if discrete and quick, is relatively isolated.
If the players try to go back to White Water or City of the Eagles for support, they will be captured by Goblins or highway-men and brought back.  Lax security inside the goblin caves will allow them a chance to rush their captors and get free.
Once the characters have entered the Central Tower, an alarm can be raised, which will ramp up the pressure on the characters to race through the building, get the rod and the Baron’s son.  They will appear trapped by the gaurds milling about in the yard, and the 1st level of the tower.  Xorn will flee in secret; the guards are too afraid to enter the upper levels of the central tower.  The characters best escape is through the under-dungeon tunnels.
