##################
  Combat Options
##################

Players sometimes want their characters to do some pretty fancy
maneuvers during combat. This chapter offer guidance with several
common ones.

|use_opend6| See the :external:ref:`OpenD6 Fantasy "Combat Options"<fantasy.combat_options>` chapter.

We'll present an additional, new topic in this chapter: poisoning

Poisoning
=========

Poisons -- also called vitriols in this world -- can be an effective supplement to bashing opponents with various kinds of weapons or enchantments.
Poisoning an opponent has a number of difficulties.

Most of this world's poisons don't have long shelf-lives.
They must be fresh to be fully lethal.

Poisons require a great deal of care in handling, otherwise the poisoner could injure themselves.
Appropriate skills are required to handle and administer poisons.

Finally, a poisoning is a kind of attack, and ordinary attack and damage rolls apply.
It's entirely possible that a fresh, properly handled poison may still fail to work against someone with a powerful physique who can shrug off the attack with only an upset stomach and a bad headache.

Since these are all difficulty rules, the players can apply various techniques to mitigate the difficulties. See :ref:`skill_difficulties` and :ref:`game_basics` for common ways to improve the needed skill rolls.

Access to Vitriols
------------------

Vitriols are created by alchemist mages who -- discretely -- specialize in poisons.
Ordinary rat poisons are a common, but moderately expensive item.
More sophisticated poisons, however, are weapons and can be difficult to come by.
Often, they involve rare or expensive ingredients.
The rarity of ingredients means the alchemist may not have a lot of experience making the poison in the first place, raising the difficulties and leading to failed skill rolls after spending a lot of money for ingredients that won't make a cat puke.

Vitriols don't rely on enchantments to operate.
Creating them requires mage-like training to be able to perform the operations required to create a working poison.
There may be enchantments over the workspace to do the required alchemical operations.
Once created, a poison isn't magical and cannot be detected as such.

There are two broad classes of vitriols: those which must be ingested, and those which need to be insinuated through skin contact.
Ingested poisons must be eaten (or drunk).
Insinuated poisons can be used on weapons, but also -- in some cases -- secretly placed on personal items like jewelry.


Ingested Vitriols
------------------

Vitriols intended for ingestion don't keep well.
They must be fresh and packaged in a way that the person mixing it into food or drink isn't injured while handling the poison.

When a poison is consumed without being mixed into food or beverage, it's undiluted, and there's no need for an attack roll.
(A GM might want to make a roll to see if there's a critical failure.)
Consuming a poison directly is usually the result of force or skills like *persuasion* to coerce someone to drink (or eat) the poison directly.
The damage will occur after the onset period.

Otherwise, the poison must be mixed into a consumable.
See the :ref:`character_basics` chapter for the *poison delivery* skill, which is helpful for proper handling of poisons.
There are two skill rolls required.
First, a skill roll my the alchemist to apply the poison.
See the :ref:`character_basics` chapter for the *poison delivery* skill, which is helpful for proper handling of poisons.
Second, an attack roll when the poison makes contact with the target.

The intended victim may make an Acumen *alchemy* skill roll to detect the presence of the poison mixed into food or drink.
Each poison has a distinct base detection difficulty. A failure means the soup tastes funny, nothing more.

Here are some adjustments to detecting an ingestible poison.

..  admonition:: Poison Concealment

    ..  csv-table::
        :header-rows: 1

        Technique, Difficulty Adjustment
        Spicy food or drink, +2
        Expertise in cooking (or mixing drinks), +1D
        Haste or Stealth required, -1D

Once ingested, the poison attack roll is made using the target's passive defense value of 10.
The number of dice for this roll is based on the skill of the character who mixed the poison plus the Result Points from the skill roll used to blend the poison into the consumable.

At the end each period of "shelf life", the poison's effect drops by 1D and the detection difficulty also drops by 5. This means that Spider poison, after one month will do 1D damage and after two months will do no damage.


Other skills, like *persuasion*, may help a victim ingest a poison that's easily detected.

Here are descriptions of some of the poisons available.

Spider Poison
~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Extracted from Giant Spiders. This isn't very complicated, and the spiders are dismayingly common in mountainous areas, making this poison relatively inexpensive.
They often dwell in caves and narrow passes through rocky canyons,
eating goats and other large wildlife.

:Onset Time: 1D+2 minutes
:Damage: 2D
:Detection Difficulty: Difficult (16-20)
:Shelf Life: 1 month

Centipede Poison
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The giant centipedes roam the edges of the deserts, preying on donkeys and camels. The pincers will knock an animal senseless, doing 3D damage from the poison, plus an additional 2D from the sword-like bite.

:Onset Time: 1D minutes
:Damage: 3D
:Detection Difficulty: Moderate (11-15)
:Shelf Life: 3 weeks

Ghoulish Flower
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

As ghouls are rumored to feed on the dead, so the Ghoul Flower is said to sprout over mass graves from ancient battles.
A rare plant is the basis for a poison called the Ghoulish Flower because a flower is said to sprout from the someone killed by this poison.
The plant is found  deep in saltwater marshes, and can only be harvested at low tide when it's visible.

:Onset Time: 1D minutes
:Damage: 4D+2
:Detection Difficulty: Easy (6-10)
:Shelf Life: 1 month

Water Snake
~~~~~~~~~~~

Poisonous water snakes are rare, but terrifying in inland waterways.
These are large (3-5m long) snakes with a powerful venom in it's fangs.
They rarely venture out of the water, but will attack animals stooping to drink from rivers.
With a chain and a massive iron hook, these snakes can be capture alive and milked for venom.

:Onset Time: 1 minute
:Damage: 2D+2 per round for 2 rounds
:Detection Difficulty: Very Difficult (21-25)
:Shelf Life: 1D days

Wyvern or Earth Dragon
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The Earth Dragons come from a Realm of Caves and Earth, also said to be inhabited by dwarvish people. Some mages believe that free earth elementals may roam around this realm, also.

These creatures are found in deep caverns. They're capable of limited flight, and emerge only to hunt. They have an acidic spit that can be distilled into a powerful poison, if it can be captured.
It required carefully made wax-lined containers to hold the spit.
The gaseous reek is damaging to lungs and eyes, and requires great care to make lenses and breathing apparatus to collect the spit.

:Onset Time: 1D+2 × 10 min.
:Damage: 3D per minute for 3 minutes
:Detection Difficulty: Moderate (11-15)
:Shelf Life: 1 week

Insinuated Vitriols
--------------------

Insinuated vitriols are put onto things like weapons so they can be inserted through wounds. They can also be put onto some every-day objects, leading injury through contact with the poisoned objects.
Some poisons -- if applied properly -- may be used multiple times.
The service life shows how long the poison will remain active.
At the end of the life, the effect is reduced by 1D.

As with ingested poisons mixed into food or drink, there are two rolls required.
First, a skill roll my the alchemist to apply the poison.
See the :ref:`character_basics` chapter for the *poison delivery* skill, which is helpful for proper handling of poisons.
Second, an attack roll when the poison makes contact with the target.

Applying a vitriol has a difficulty of 16-20, Difficult.
The GM can apply adjustments based on the situation;
the player may try to mitigate these problems using related skills, or extra time in preparation.
Make this skill roll first, to see if the poison is applied properly.

Add 2 to the Result Points from the skill roll to get the number of dice in the vitriol's subsequent attack rolls.
This will be used when the poison finally makes contact with the target to see if it actually works.

Failure to apply a poison can have any one of a variety of outcomes.

-   Injury to the alchemist applying the poison. (Alchemist takes 1D damage; poison still works.)
-   Poison's effect reduced by 1D.
-   Difficulty in detect reduced by 5.
-   Service life reduced by :math:`\tfrac{1}{2}`.
-   The difficulty of the poison working moves from automatic to Easy, and a skill roll is required.
-   Poison's attack roll is -1D.

The intended victim may make an Acumen *alchemy* skill roll to detect the presence of the poison on a weapon on an object they're handling.
Each poison has a distinct base detection difficulty.
A failure means the object feels funny or the weapon blade looks funny, nothing more.

For poisoned weapons, the weapon must first penetrate armor and do damage.
This is ordinary combat and ordinary damage that must happen first.
For other insinuations, where the target simply touches the poison with their unprotected skin, there's no weapon attack roll needed.

The poison will then perform a second attack roll to the resulting wound or exposed skin.
Unless some enchantment is in effect, this is against a passive defense value of 10.
The number of dice in the poison's attack is 2 plus the result points from the skill roll in applying the poison.

Biting Oil
~~~~~~~~~~~

A secretive recipe that seems to be known to a alchemists.
This seems to be made from common elements including aqua fortis and brimstone.

:Onset Time: 1D+2 minutes
:Damage: 1D
:Detection Difficulty: Moderate
:Service Life: 1 week
:Uses: 2 uses

Acid Grease
~~~~~~~~~~~

A version of Giant Spider poison that is mixed with certain tree saps to make a greasy coating for weapons.

:Onset Time: 1D minutes
:Damage: 2D per minute for 2 minutes
:Detection Difficulty: Difficult
:Service Life: 2D days
:Uses: 1D dose

Paralyzing Wax
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Made from the waxy residue on the leaves of rare desert shrubs.

:Onset Time: 1D rounds
:Damage: 2D
:Detection Difficulty: Very Difficult
:Service Life: 4D days
:Uses: 1D

Curare
~~~~~~~

Made from special plant bark.

:Onset Time: 1 round
:Damage: 3D
:Detection Difficulty: Difficult
:Service Life: 1 Day
:Uses: 1
