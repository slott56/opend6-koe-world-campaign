##################
 Non-Human Races
##################

This chapter provides information on some of the other humanoid races common in fantasy settings.

|mod_opend6| See the :external:ref:`OpenD6 Fantasy "Non-Human Races" <fantasy.non_human>` chapter.

Background
==========

Dwarves, Elves, Gnomes (also known as Goblins, and Kobolds) are seen in this realm as a consequence of travel between realms.
Related vaguely humanoid creatures like Ogres and Trolls, similarly, are denizens of some other realm.
These creatures are not native to this realm, and cannot long survive here.
Some are here on their own quests, others are trapped here.

The primary mode of travel between realms is described in :ref:`options.special_abilities_teleportation`.
Travel between realms is the heart of all *apportation*-related magic.
For more information, see :ref:`world.magic`, specifically, the Apportation skill.

..  admonition:: Other Realms and Other Races

    Other-realm creatures like dark elves or goblins often want to avoid sunlight because their alternate realm has different light.
    They find our intense sun dangerous and possibly damaging.
    Similarly, we'd find their gloomy realm a difficult place to exist in.

When attempting to use any *apportation*-related enchantment, including *teleportation* special ability, there is a strong possibility of encountering  other-worldly creatures.
There are several ways magical creatures can leak into our realm from their own. These processes include:

-   Random occurrences including an inter-realm rift opening near them.
    This may correspond with phases of the moon or the positions of other planets. It may be related to comets or meteors.

-   Some magical monsters have been pulled here by mages.
    These creatures are part of the mage's quest, and may not be willing participants. In some cases, the creatures expect to find the means for wealth or power here.

-   Monsters may be pushed here by mages in other realms for reasons that may be hard for us to understand.
    It may involve punishing the creature.
    It may involve punishing some mage in this realm.

-   The *Teleportation* special ability may arise as a genetic mutation in some creatures, permitting transfers among realms.

There are other possibilities, of course.
There are innumerable reasons for moving between realms.

The more intelligent races (Intellect ≥ 3D, including ogres, dwarves, elves, more intelligent gnomes) have their own mages, and those mages can also manipulate the veil between the realms.
The less intelligent races (Intellect < 3D, including giants, trolls, less intelligent gnomes) are often moved around by mages and used as weapons.

**Example**.
An enchantment for "demonic summoning" uses Apportation as well as a Psychic Communication to connect with a creature from outside our realm.
Generally, djinni, and afreeti, and similar creatures  labeled as demons are actually very powerful mages from other realms.
This means that a device like a lamp for "summoning" a djinni might actually be planted by a djinn who's waiting for help to open the veil between worlds.
