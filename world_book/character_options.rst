Cultural Notes
--------------

The Kingdom of the East is a violent culture, with all of the common ways to show force and dominate others.
There are numerous -- and often subtle -- badges of rank and social standing.
These all manifest through the a few of the **Advantages**.

These are compounded by each specific Temple's unique spin on rank and power, leading to a great deal of social complexity.

..  admonition:: Wealth and Funds

    The base rules have this equivalence:

    -   Each rank of Wealth is 10 gold/month.

        This is 2.5 gold/week, 20 silver/week.

    -   Funds base is 3D, and varies with Charisma, Intellect, and Trading Skills.

        The rules say this

            "...multiply the *Funds* total by a value specified by the gamemaster (typically about 60 silver, ..."

        If we interpret this to mean 1D × 20 silver/week, this fits with Wealth ranks.

    Each 1D of Funds is R1 of Wealth.

    The Wealth advantage adds to the character's base Funds.
    For example, a skilled Squire may have Funds of 4D plus R1 of Wealth, giving them a total of 5D when making funding difficulty checks.

..  Admonition:: Coin equivalence

    "...eight copper pieces equals one silver piece; eight silver pieces equals one gold piece..."

Other **Advantages** have some cultural and social implications.

-   Equipment (R1, R2, R3, R4) the equipment is generally specialized by temple. Followers of Hildeþrymm have access to temple-blessed weapons and armor. Followers of Folme will often purchase tools through their Temple.

-   Contacts (R1, R2, R3, R4) reflects the web of obligations. This is always deference to an immediate superior of a higher rank. It's also protection for, and the ability to command, immediate inferiors of lower ranks. It also includes peer relationships among followers of a temple.

    For example, Knights often know other Knights.
    This is limited by geography: distant earldoms can't easily communicate.

-   Patron (R1, R2, R3) reflects direct patronage from superior to inferior. A loyal Apprentice has the patronage of a Journeyman training them.

-   Cultures (R1, R2, R3, R4) reflects insight into other Temples and other social hierarchies. R1 is knowledge of a specific Temple, or perhaps Outlanders, or one of the Western Empire kingdoms. R2 is knowledge of multiple Temples, or in-depth knowledge of a specific Temple, or the ability to live with Outlanders, or multiple kingdoms in the Western Empire. R3 and R4 are for people who **are** Outlanders or other foreigners living the the Kingdom of the East.

Special Abilities
=================

|mod_opend6| There are several changes.

- The definition of the *teleportation* special ability has to be modified from the definition in the Fantasy Rules: without Apportation to move things around in this realm, teleportation can't exist.

- The *shape shifting* special ability a kind of Psionic power, not a transformation of mass. It embraces similar ideas to *Possession: Full*. The important difference is that shape-shifting exchanges control of the bodies, where possession does not yield control of the possessor's body to the one being possessed.

..  _options.special_abilities_teleportation:

TELEPORTATION (3)
----------------------------------------

Teleportation special ability allows the character to move between **realms**.
There is no easy way to move things around within a realm.

Once in another realm, the character can move to a new location in that realm, at their normal movement. This is complicated by the fact that the new realm may not have the same physical structure as the realm they left.
If they are then able to reenter this realm, they will be in the new location.

Anyone with the ability to teleport will only be able to survive if they teleport to one of the small number of habitable realms. At the R1, they know exactly one realm, at higher ranks, they can either know additional realms or reduce the difficulty of opening a gateway they know by 3 (but not both.)

For more details on inter-realm movement, see the Apportation section of the :ref:`world.magic` chapter.

..  admonition:: Teleportation Details

    Teleportation can involve a sequence of as many as three difficulty checks:

    1.  Opening a gateway for themselves to another realm.
        The essential difficulty is 15 to open a gateway.
        Rank 1 of the apportation skill means the character know one realm.
        Higher ranks can either know more realms or reduce the difficulty to open a gateway.

    2.  Moving in the other realm is a matter of not getting lost in the other realm's physical layout, and not encountering any of the creatures inhabiting the target realm.
        This is up to the GM, and may require successful skill rolls for searching, navigating, and possibly avoiding detection.

    3.  Opening a gateway back to this realm.
        The essential difficulty is still 15 because this is a previously visited realm.

    The character may take along whatever they can carry.

    Failure can occur in any of these three steps.
    See the Apportation section of :ref:`world.magic` for details.

    -   Failure to open a gateway to another realm means nothing appears to happen.

    -   Failure to move successfully in the other realm can stem from failing to navigate the geography (resulting in 1D damage) or an encounter with an inhabitant of the other realm.

    -   Failure to return to this realm.
        This is rarely permanent, but can be distressing
        A critical failure may include 2D damage or  an encounter with an inhabitant.

    Death will return the character's mortal remains to this realm.
    If someone is available to attempt resuscitation, they might survive this extra-dimensional death.
    See the :ref:`healing` section for more details.

..  _`options.special_abilities.shapeshifting`:

SHAPESHIFTING (3)
----------------------------------------

The shapeshifting power is a psionic ability to seize control of some other body.
The character's intent -- their spirits -- trade places.
When taking control of a large animal, the animal is likely to be terrified and confused in the shape-shifter's original body.
The animal's spirit may -- after an adjustment period of an hour or more -- try to use the shape-shifter's human form. A fish or aquatic mammal might start looking for water. A bird or or air-born creature might try to fly away.

The shapeshifter can use Charisma-based skills to try and calm the creature in their body. They are in intimate contact, and communication of essential feelings like fear and comfort are possible.

The rank determines the difference in mass between the the shape-shifter and target bodies. At Rank 1, a shapeshifting human can switch bodies with a creature from 40Kg to 160Kg: from a the size of a dog to the size of a lion.
At Rank 2, the range expands from 16Kg large reptile to a 400kg large bear or alligator.

See the :ref:`fantasy.magic.benchmarks` table for the way the sizes scale up (and down) with rank.

..  csv-table::
    :header-rows: 1

    Rank, Benchmark Range, Mass, Benchmark Examples
    1, 8-11, "40Kg-160Kg", Dog to Lion
    2, 6-13, "16Kg-400Kg", Sea Turtle to Alligator
    3, 4-15, "6.3Kg-1,000Kg", Armadillo to Rhinoceros
    4, 2-17, "2.5Kg-2,500Kg", Goose to Hippopotamus
    5, 1-19, "1.6Kg-6,300Kg", Owl to Elephant
    6, 0-20, "1kg-10,000Kg", Raven to Orca or Small Dragon

The target creature must be within sight of the person attempting to shape-shift and control the creature.
The shape-shifter will require Intellect and Coordination skills to master a new body.
Non-mammalian bodies will be particularly challenging: the way lungs and gullets work may be alarming to a shape-shifter, the first time they attempt it.
The GM may require numerous skill checks the first time a shapeshifter approaches a new kind of creature.
Swimming and Flying, in particular, are nearly impossible without appropriate skills, and may lead to spectacular failures.
