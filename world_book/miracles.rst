..  _miracles:

############
  Miracles
############

Miracles are invocations performed by priests of any of the six temples: Cyþan, Hildeþrymm, Witan, Folme, Hælan, or Bealu.
|mod_opend6| See the :external:ref:`OpenD6 Fantasy, "Miracles"<fantasy.miracles>` for some background.
Because most tables lack the kinds of skilled training and scholarship the temple of Cyþan offers to mages, the miracles offered by priests of other temples tend be less spectacular in their effects than enchantments.

Miracles are generally focused on a character's skills and attributes, as well as divination.
There are few examples with complicated effects based on conjuration.

The Basis of Miracles
=====================

Are actual deities involved?
Perhaps not.

One explanation is that the miracles are simpler, easier-to-perform enchantments.

Other explanation is the beings revered as deities are beings from other realms.
They can can be coerced into intervening in events in this realm.
A miracle is a kind of Apportation magic; the priest is working with a being from another realm.

Background: Religion and Temples
===================================

There are six principle temples.
The first five are public facing.
The sixth, Baelu, tends to be secretive and obscure.

Hildeþrymm
----------

Attribute is Physique and related skills.
Temperament is Sanguine.

Their archetypes are Fighters, Warders, Wanderers.

Each temple is essentially a dojo: clean floors, weapons stacked in frames. Some damage to walls and ceilings from weapons practice.

They tend to be very afraid of traps and snares.

Their preferred games involve Dice.

Mythology has Hildeþrymm playing at dice guarding the gates to the world. Other gods get in. Her moment of inattention let Bealu into the human realm.

..  admonition:: Religion Worksheet: Temple of Hildeþrymm

    **Symbols**

    Shields and swords.
    Ideally, **only** knights carry swords, which mete justice.
    Most others use hammers, axes, spears, etc.

    **Tenets**

    Their focus is on duty and oaths. A promise to love kindness, seek justice, and walk with humility. An oath by Hildeþrymm to provide rest by the welcoming fire in the Great Hall for those who die in service.

    Common phrases include:

    - “Hildeþrymm sets traps for the unwary”

    - “Keep your swords sharp”.

    - “We receive you into our protection."

    - "You must know your target before your sword does any good."

    **Treatment of Those of Not of the Sam Religion/God/Power**

    Protect them, guide them, if possible.

    They form the backbone of an army, also,
    and can be cruel.

    **Relationship to Nature**

    They travel widely on and off the royal highways.
    They hunt and forage to maintain an army.

    **Food Restrictions**

    None. They'll eat what's put in front of them. And they tend to wolf a meal down very quickly.

    **Killing**

    Anyone, as needed. Killing criminals in the name of justice is common.

    **Invocation Requirements**

    :Community: Permitted
    :Components: Religious symbol: Sword (uncommon) (-4)
    :Concentration: For the duration of the casting time (varies)
    :Countenance: Not used
    :Feedback: Not used
    :Gesture: Complex sword movements (-3)
    :Incantation: Litany (-4)

Cyþan
-----

Attribute is Intellect and related skills.
Temperament is Melancholic.

Their archetypes are Mages and Alchemists.

A mage temple has high towers and large forges.
Recently, this has shifted to smaller, more portable forges, upsetting a delicate balance of power among the temples and the earls.

Any given temple has three parts: the forge, the library, and the hall.
They tend to preserve items and artifacts way past when they're useful.
Often, they're cluttered with carefully preserved artifacts where any provenance or meaning is long forgotten.
Temples are built by adding rather than replacing or rebuilding.
This means there will be buildings called the "Old Library" and the "New Library".
In the very large temples, the "new library" hasn't been used for generations, and other naming conventions will be adopted.
Perhaps the "North library", or the "Spring Library"

Mages tend to be very afraid of two things: seeing the future and raising the dead.
These are powers they have but can’t discern how to control.

Their preferred game is any kind of trick-taking Card game. They love cards.

Mythology has Cyþan studying, and learning; failing to respond quickly enough to stop Bealu from breaking through a portal into the human realm.


..  admonition:: Religion Worksheet: Temple of Cyþan

    **Symbols**

    Their symbols include hexagons and honeycombs.
    The forge often requires books with incantations.
    Depending on what they're doing, there may also be tools like tongs and ladles.
    Their prayers are burned in a symbolic forge.

    **Tenets**

    Their focus is a promise of power through certain knowledge.

    Common phrases are

    -   “Sweet Cyþan lift us out of ignorance”

    -   “Cyþan guide us to knowledge.”

    -   “I know you, I would know your words.”

    **Treatment of Those of Not of the Same Religion/God/Power**

    A cold disdain. Most people come to mages for help, the mages are often unable to help.
    The forge is expensive and complex, and peoples' needs for magical intervention and their offerings must match the cost of operation.
    And, of course, the request must be within the power of the mages.

    **Relationship to Nature**

    They consume resources recklessly: coal, soft pitch pine, ore for forging metal implements.

    **Food Restrictions**

    A very complex seasonal diet to keep their internal vibrations in tune with the realm, the elements, and the eternal æther.

    **Killing**

    Reluctantly. Killing is the job of the followers of Hildeþrymm.

    **Invocation Requirements**

    :Community: Optional
    :Components: Religious symbol: books, tongs, ladles (uncommon) (-4)
    :Concentration: For the duration of the casting time (varies)
    :Countenance: Not used
    :Feedback: Damage resistance lowered by 2 (-2)
    :Gesture: Ritual dance (complex) -3
    :Incantation: Litany (-4)

Witan
-----

Attribute is Acumen and related skills.
Temperament is Phlegmatic.

Character archetypes are Traders, Merchants, Counselors, Shopkeepers, and Hostelers. Also, to a lesser extent, Fisherman, Foresters, and Hunters, but not Farmers.

Their houses often have a shrine to the departed with incense in bowls.

They're afraid of unwatched fire, any fire, no matter how small.
There's a profound anxiety over loss and financial setbacks.

Their preferred games are territory games like Checkers, Chess, Go, and similar games of acquisition.

**Mythology**: Witan unwittingly aided Bealu; Witan helps everyone.

..  admonition:: Religion Worksheet: Temple of Witan

    **Symbols**

    Their symbols is a begging bowl.

    **Tenets**

    They prefer to create kitchens for feeding the hungry, the lost, and the displaced.
    The followers of this temple tend to set wealth aside for the poor and the hard times.
    They value a disciplined life that includes service to the lost and damaged.

    Common phrases are

    -   "Witan helps everyone, good and bad alike."

    -   "I beg your pardon."

    **Treatment of Those of Not of the Same Religion/God/Power**

    Casual disregard. While Hælen's priests will help with injury and life-threatening diseases, they don't help with sustenance. Hildeþrymm's followers engage in fighting that displaces people. The Cyþan temple demands gold for everything.

    **Relationship to Nature**

    They tolerate farming: they prefer hunting and foraging.
    Nature must be tended, lightly, to encourage production of plants and animals that are helpful to everyone.

    **Food Restrictions**

    None. You don't turn down an offering.

    **Killing**

    Where necessary. In big cities, the worst criminals are turned over to the temple of Hildeþrymm for execution.
    A tiny village may not have a temple of Hildeþrymm, and the priests of Witan will execute a particularly egregious criminal.

    **Invocation Requirements**

    :Community: Permitted
    :Components: Religious symbol: Begging Bowl (-3)
    :Concentration: For the duration of the casting time (varies)
    :Countenance: Not used
    :Feedback: Not used
    :Gesture: Complex gestures (-3)
    :Incantation: Litany (-4)


Folme
-----

Attribute is Agility.
Temperament is Choleric.

The character archetypes are folks with technical skills: tinker, smith, farrier, luthier, weaver, crafter, farmer, carpenter.

Their temples are often little more than a modified shed. There will be tools and containers hanging on the walls everywhere; complex shrines with boxes and a *sanctum* and sometimes a *sanctum sanctorum* cabinet.

They're afraid of losing or misplacing their tools.

Their preferred game is Dominoes, a mixture of luck and some strategy.

**Mythology**: Folme didn’t build a secure enough gateway to control access to this world. A flaw in their craftsmanship brought woe into the world.

..  admonition:: Religion Worksheet: Temple of Folme

    **Symbols**

    Their symbols are an apron, a hammer, and a book of hours.

    **Tenets**

    Skilled work takes training and time.
    Talent helps, but isn't essential.
    They value the master-apprentice relationship.

    They know Folme makes blessings as well as curses.
    Not all gifts from Folme are beneficial.

    A common phrase is "Folme gives and Folme takes."

    **Treatment of Those of Not of the Same Religion/God/Power**

    They'll practice their craft for anyone with money to cover their labor and materials.

    **Relationship to Nature**

    Exploitive: they grab a lot of raw materials.
    They insist it's the role of followers of Witan to manage the resources.

    **Food Restrictions**

    Few.  They tend to prefer things that are a bit more complicated and require technical skills to make: bread and anything of the pasties/empanadas/savory pie family that involves multiple kinds of cooking.

    **Killing**

    As needed. Justice doesn't happen by accident, it's created and maintained.

    **Invocation Requirements**

    :Community: Prohibited
    :Components: Religious symbol: Apron, Hammer, Book (common) (-3)
    :Concentration: For the duration of the casting time (varies)
    :Countenance: Not used
    :Feedback: Permitted
    :Gesture: Complex gestures (-3)
    :Incantation: Litany (-4)

Hælan
-----

Central attribute is Coordination.
Temperament is Phlegmatic.

Archetypes are Healers, Animal Handlers, Drovers, and Sailors.

A Hælan temple is generally, a hospital, clinic, or veterinary service.
They also manage caravans of animals because of the high degree of coordination and animal husbandry required.

Their places are super clean.
It's common to see water buckets and butts everywhere to wash in.
They're afraid of contamination from dirt or people with communicable diseases.

The prefer counter-based games like Mankala and Owari.

**Mythology**: Hælan has been trying to ease the suffering brought about by misuse and abuse of others.

..  admonition:: Religion Worksheet: Temple of Hælan

    **Symbols**

    Their symbols is a spur.

    [Spur? hæle (feminine) is health, hæla (masculine) is “heel”. It’s a kind of pun in the old language.]

    **Tenets**

    You can't get anywhere without coordination of multiple people.
    Sailing a ship, managing a caravan are their strong suits.
    Any kind of travel over a long distance will often have a priest of Hælan to make sure they have what they need and also to make sure no one gets severely injured along the way.

    **Treatment of Those of Not of the Same Religion/God/Power**

    Life is service to others.

    **Relationship to Nature**

    Distant. They're focused on people, their organizations, and their health. Nature serves their needs.

    **Food Restrictions**

    Scrupulously clean. Soups that have been boiled.
    Wine and beer that's made from boiled water.
    Distilled spirits that are doubly-clean.

    Animals must be ritually killed and bled to be sure they're as clean as possible.

    **Killing**

    **Invocation Requirements**

    :Community: Prohibited
    :Components: Tool currently being used (-3)
    :Concentration: For the duration of the casting time (varies)
    :Countenance: Not used
    :Feedback: Damage resistance lowered by 3 (-3)
    :Gesture: Simple gestures (-2)
    :Incantation: Litany (-4)

Bealu
-----

They're focused on Charisma.
Temperament is Sanguine.

The character archetypes are actors, grifters, and thieves.

There are numerous secretive sects that follow Bealu.
Some are simply secretive.
A few pretend to some other god as a shield to protect Bealu from unbelievers.
Some sects do not even realize they're occult followers of Bealu.

They're often afraid of running afoul of the other organized templates.
Since Bealu followers are about expedience, they cut corners and ignore the everyday rules the other temples tend to impose.

They don't have a preferred game. They're looking for an edge in anyone else's game: loaded dice, fake card shuffling, tricky opening moves in other games.

**Mythology**: Bealu stands in opposition to Witan. Bealu brings chaos into the world; Witan brings the order of the eternal æther.
Chaos includes free-will and fear as well as growth and evolutionary change.

..  admonition:: Religion Worksheet: Temple of Bealu

    **Symbols**

    Their symbols center around flame, and sometimes a whip.

    **Tenets**

    Bealu is the trickster god, embodying the power of chaos.
    It's said Bealu's followers treasure chaos and greed, but this isn't strictly true.
    They generally value finding the most expedient means to an end.

    **Treatment of Those of Not of the Same Religion/God/Power**

    Useful pawns.

    **Relationship to Nature**

    None. The natural world is theirs to exploit.

    **Food Restrictions**

    They prefer something elaborately showy.
    They'll sometimes delight in wrecking a complicated desert.

    **Killing**

    They'll kill casually to achieve their ends.

    **Invocation Requirements**

    :Community: Prohibited
    :Components: Religious symbol: varies with sect, generally common (-3)
    :Concentration: For the duration of the casting time (varies)
    :Countenance: Disheveled, noticeable (-1)
    :Feedback: Damage resistance lowered by 3 (-3)
    :Gesture: Simple and offensive (-3)
    :Incantation: An offensive sentence (-3)


..  admonition:: Priests and Ministers

    Note there is distinction between two parts of a temple's members.
    There is a priesthood that serves within the temple.
    Separately, there are those members elected to go out into the world to minister to people outside the temple, the worldly ministers.

    These external ministers define the principle archetypes for characters associated with given temples.

    -   Hildeþrymm Warriors,
    -   Cyþan Mages,
    -   Witan Merchants and Adjuvants,
    -   Folme Crafters,
    -   Hælan Physicians and Animal Handlers,
    -   Baelu's various secret societies tend to infiltrate the worldly ministers from other temples.


Mythology
-----------

The cosmos has two domains: above is the eternal æther, the domain of order and things, while below is void, the domain of chaos.
In the beginning, these two domains are inhabited by Witan and Bælu.

Witan can see the eternal æther has four elemental aspects.
Fire is assigned to Cyþan. The earth is given to Hildeþrymm. Air is the province of Folme. The waters are allocated to Hælan.

To understand this, consider the six faces of a die.
(Or the six vertices of an Octohedron.)
One of the sides represents order, and there labors Witan.
The opposite side represents chaos, and Baelu will be found there.
The remaining four sides have a progression to them.
Fire begets earth begets water begets air.
We find Cyþan's labors providing material for Hildeþrymm.
The products of these labors supply Folme, who, in their turn,
supplies Hælan.
Looking at the die, it's clear Fire and Water eternally opposed; similarly, Air and Water are also in opposition.

Obtaining Access to Miracles
============================

See the :external:ref:`OpenD6 Fantasy "Miracles"<fantasy.miracles>` chapter.

All characters who want to have the Miracles Extranormal attribute must have at least one rank each in the Devotion and Employed Disadvantages, both related to their specific temple.
Neither of these count toward the character's maximum number of Disadvantages.

The player can then put dice in the Miracles attribute and skills at character creation.
The costs are the same as obtaining other attributes.

When player gives a character the Miracles attribute, the player must
declare what specific temple the character belongs to.
A character may never have multiple versions of the Miracles attribute, nor may they have any skill, Advantage, or Disadvantage based on some other temple.

Additionally, each character has **Piety** points that can help with an invocation.

Learning and Improving Miracle Skills
======================================

See the :external:ref:`OpenD6 Fantasy "Miracles"<fantasy.miracles>` chapter.

The costs are the same as for other skills.
The costs are **not** doubled as in some other variant **OpenD6** systems.

Extranormal Skills
===================

See the :external:ref:`OpenD6 Fantasy "Miracles"<fantasy.miracles>` chapter.

For the most part, the Favor and Strife skills are two subclasses of the Temperamental Alteration skill.
The Divination is equivalent between mages and priests.

Note that a Favor skill can also include elements of Temperamental Conjuration as a way to practice a limited form of necromancy.

Also, see :ref`magic` for more information on Psychic Communication, which is part of some miracles.

Characteristics of a Miracle
=================================

As with enchantments, there are two parts to an invocation.

1. Establish a link.
    Unlike Enchantments, cover is not a defense against an Invocation.
    The Variable Movement aspect doesn't really have any meaning.

2. Work the effect using the link.

Remember that players may spend Character and Fate Points to make
sure they have sufficiently high skill totals to establish a link or create the effect (or both).

..  admonition:: INVOCATION SUCCESS LEVELS

    **Minimal (0-2)**: The miracle meets the needs of the devotee while disturbing the environment as little as possible.
    It would be hard to convince anyone that it wasn't simply coincidence.

    **Good (3-6)**: The miracle not only happens as desired, there is some small side effect that divine intervention happened.
    The effect depends on the type of miracle and the nature of the divinity called upon.

    **Superior (7-12)**: The miracle occurs much better than hoped, and the devotee gains some additional benefit.

    **Spectacular (13 or more)**: There is no question that divine intervention caused the miracle to occur.
    The results are well beyond what the devotee requested, though not more than the situation needs.

Piety and Miracles
--------------------------

Piety is required to perform miracles.
This is tracked somewhat like character points.

A character starts with a piety of zero points.
Virtuous acts will increase a character's piety.
Sinful acts will reduce a character's piety.

Piety points can be spent to purchase additional dice when performing an invocation.
The points can **also** be used to purchase a character point to be used for other difficulty rolls: 5 piety points can be used to purchase one character point that can be used for any other purpose.

At higher ranks, a single sinful act will lose piety based on the sin plus, the character's rank as the reduction.

-----------------------------

Each temple has basic tenets. A miracle that does
not conform to those tenets suffers a modifier to its difficulty.

    ..  csv-table::

Invocation Design
==================

Start with effects. Add positive and negative modifiers.

Required Aspects
----------------
Miracles share some of the same aspects as enchantments: effect, duration, range, speed, and casting time.

Invocations also involve one or more limiting factors, as dictated by the religion.
The cleric does gain the benefit from adding these limitations to the miracle.
Clerics must include the required aspects in any miracle they perform, though, unless the religion mandates otherwise, they don't need to include all of them.

Required aspects include: community, components, concentration, countenance, feedback, gestures, and incantation.

Disallowed Aspects
------------------

A miracle may not include charges (except as a ward) or unreal effects.
The Variable Movement aspect isn't useful for miracles because cover is not a defense.

Generally, gamemasters should disallow the "other alterants" aspects; these additional features aren't appropriate for miracles.

Aspect Specifics
-----------------

Although miracles can involve most Magic spell aspects as is, a handful have restrictions or modifications.

Casting Time
~~~~~~~~~~~~

Casting time varies quite a bit from a 1.5 second shield to a five-minute ritual of purification.
A cleric can develop an alternate form of an incantation that takes more time, reducing the difficulty.

Charges
~~~~~~~

Not used in general. A ward, however, is permitted.

Community
~~~~~~~~~

Some temples permit community, some prohibit it.
The community modifier can simplify a spell for followers of certain templates.

Components, Gestures, and Incantations
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The components are -- almost universally -- at least one of the religious symbols for the cleric's temple.
These are often carried, or worn, as part of the cleric's clothing.
For the heavy implements of Folme, a smaller version is used by a cleric.

Additionally, some invocations will involve an additional component.
This might be food, a tree, or even a recently deceased body.

The gestures are specific to each temple, not each invocation.
There's a short collection of gestures (or dance steps) that apply to more than one invocation.

For some temples, the gestures are complex.
For the temple of Cyþan, the gesture is a ritual dance around the forge, or the sources of the elements involved.

Similarly, the incantations are specific to the temple.
The melodies (and harmonies) and specific tuning pitches are central.
The words are a way to help the priest focus the effect; they are not in some lost language, and not the source of the power.
They chant is a mnemonic form of a map of the spiritual journey the priest is invoking.

Countenance
~~~~~~~~~~~

This is generally applied to invocations in the name of Bealu.
This balances the simplicity of the gestures and incantations.

Feedback
~~~~~~~~

Feedback in magic results from the mage being wrestling with elemental transformations with a balance so sensitive that it exposes the mage to harm.
Generally, invocations involve less profound elemental transformations, avoiding risk.

A few temples have ways to reduce the difficulty of an invocation by permitting feedback to the priest.

Variable Movement
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Since concealment is not a defense, this is not useful.

Other Conditions
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Conditions that limit miracles are rare.
There are a few conditions that commonly appear in invocations.

-   Limited to humans is the most common.

-   Prevention from doing harm is also used sometimes.

-   Limited to humanoids (including undead) of a different temple is used for the **Banish** invocation.


Minimum Difficulty
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The minimum difficulty for any invocation is 5.

Design Time
-----------

A character needs time to perfect a new invocation.
Each rank of difficulty doubles the number of days required.

..  csv-table::

    difficulty, rank, time
    1-5, 1, 1 day
    6-10, 2, 2 days
    11-15, 3, 4 days
    16-20, 4, 8 days
    21-25, 5, 18 days
    26-30, 6, 32 days

**Example**. A new enchantment with a difficulty of 12 will have a rank of 4. This requires 8 days of steady work to perfect.

Characters revising or modifying a invocation they previously designed will reduce the complexity and cut their time in half.
Starting from a similar invocation in a book or on a scroll can also reduce the time by a rank, cutting the time in half.

===================

They're broken into ranks, depending on the skills of the Priest.
