Adventure Tips¶

The key means of play in a roleplaying game is the adventure. This chapter is devoted to some tips on preparing and running adventures, including obstacle ideas, rewards, and generic characters.

The OpenD6 Fantasy rules can be used without modification. See the OpenD6 Fantasy, “Adventure Tips” chapter.

This section adds some unique monsters, and a unique view of monstrous life and how monsters come to inhabit the edges of the characters’ world.

Generic Monsters¶

Generally, the OpenD6 Fantasy rules can be used with some modifications. Not all of the fantasy creatures fit well with this world. Elements of these monsters descriptions are copied from the OpenD6 Fantasy, “Adventure Tips” chapter.

Most “monsters” are creatures from other realms. There are three avenues for moving between realms.

  • The creature has magic-using attributes and is able to open the veil using some kind of apportion enchantment or special ability.

  • The creature has used Psychic Communication to tempt a character in this realm to open the veil, allowing (or forcing) the creature through. A mage might ensnare a Djinn and imprison it in a container in this realm, for example.

  • Some magic-using third-party creature, like an Ogre, has forced a monster through the veil to the human realm.

In many cases, monsters may lack a ready mechanism to open the veil and return. A band of goblins, or a nest or trolls may be living in this realm – and raising havoc – because they can’t return. They may be very angry at the trickery that abandoned them here. Or, it’s entirely possible they have a way back and are in this realm exploiting the natural resources.

The GM is advised to use these monsters with a little care. They have not been edited to include any realm-specific details.

Unique Monsters¶

These monsters are unique to this world. They’re grouped by the realm from which they originate.

They’re found in this realm, in places where “the veil is thin”. These are often spectacular vistas of mountains or deserts or the surging ocean.

In other cases, these are places where some powerful mage, using extranormal powers, has weakened the veil.

Ælvish Realm of Mountains and Forests¶

This is almost indistinguishable from the human realm. Things are somewhat different: perhaps the trees are further apart, or perhaps the water is more sparkling. The air seems a bit more pure. A fire seems to burn more yellow with less blue. It’s notable for the lack of actual humans.
