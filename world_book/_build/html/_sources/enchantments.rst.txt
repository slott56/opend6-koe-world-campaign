..  _enchantments:

################
  Enchantments
################

Enchantments can be found in several sources.

-   The `Unique Enchantments`_ section has the enchantments that are unique to these rules.

-   The *OpenD6 Fantasy Book* has a number of spells in the `OpenD6 Fantasy Spells`_ section. The material presented in here has been modified slightly from the source to better fit this world.

-   The (unpublished) *D6 Magic Guidebook* has spells that can be used (with some modifications) as enchantments in this world.
    The `OpenD6 Magic Guide Sample Enchantments`_ section below repeats the spells from this book.

-   The *OpenD6 System Book* has several spells, also.
    The `OpenD6 System Enchantments`_ section repeats those spells.
    The chaos bindings and chaos magic are not a good fit for this world,
    and should be avoided.

For more background,
see **D6 Magic Guidebook** :external:ref:`magic_guide.precalculated` chapter for the Magic Guidebook spells in context.
Also, see **OpenD6 System Book** :external:ref:`system.spells` for some spell-related material.

Additionally, the `OpenD6 Project <https://opend6project.wordpress.com>`_ web site is a source of yet more ideas for spells.

OpenD6 Magic Guide Sample Enchantments
=======================================

From the *OpenD6 Magic Guidebook* there are several important sets of spells.

First, there's an "Alternate Magic Systems" chapter.
These are simple spell descriptions that seem to fit with the rules of this campaign world.
For more details, see :external:ref:`OpenD6 Magic Guidebook Alternate Magic System <magic_guide.alternate>`.

Second, there are a large number of spells organized into "schools".
We don't repeat those spells here. They're in the :external:ref:`D6 Magic Guidebook Precalculated Spells <magic_guide.precalculated>`.

The key concept from the "alternate" magic system is the free-form spells.
The players are encouraged to look for unique magical solutions to problems, not recite well-known enchantments.

OpenD6 System Enchantments
==========================

From the *OpenD6 System Book*.

These rules are in the  "Chaos Magic" section.
The idea of chaotic, uncontrolled effects doesn't fit perfectly with the system
in Kingdom of the East world book.

These rules treat magic as an act of will.
The attempt can fail for two reasons:
first, because there is no link,
and second because the mage lacks the skills to bend reality to create the desired effect.
Consequently, failure in this world generally to results in simple fatigue and reversion to the way things were before the enchantment was started.

This world does -- to an extent -- permit some chaos effects.
When mages look at the four elements, they see a decomposition of the eternal æther.
The opposite of this æther is the void, which is defined by chaos.
Chaos is the absence of the essential ætheric elements.
More importantly, it's the absence of any principle for the ordering of those elements.
It's an absence of the kind of balance that makes life possible.
