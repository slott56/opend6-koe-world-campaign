Enchantments¶

Enchantments can be found in several sources.

  • The Unique Enchantments section has the enchantments that are unique to these rules.

  • The OpenD6 Fantasy Book has a number of spells in the OpenD6 Fantasy Spells section. The material presented in here has been modified slightly from the source to better fit this world.

  • The (unpublished) D6 Magic Guidebook has spells that can be used (with some modifications) as enchantments in this world. The OpenD6 Magic Guide Sample Enchantments section below repeats the spells from this book.

  • The OpenD6 System Book has several spells, also. The OpenD6 System Enchantments section repeats those spells. The chaos bindings and chaos magic are not a good fit for this world, and should be avoided.

For more background, see D6 Magic Guidebook Precalculated Spells chapter for the Magic Guidebook spells in context. Also, see OpenD6 System Book Supernatural Powers for some spell-related material.

Additionally, the OpenD6 Project web site is a source of yet more ideas for spells.

OpenD6 System Enchantments¶

From the OpenD6 System Book.

These rules are in the “Chaos Magic” section. The idea of chaotic, uncontrolled effects doesn’t fit perfectly with the system in Kingdom of the East world book.

These rules treat magic as an act of will. The attempt can fail for two reasons: first, because there is no link, and second because the mage lacks the skills to bend reality to create the desired effect. Consequently, failure in this world generally to results in simple fatigue and reversion to the way things were before the enchantment was started.

This world does – to an extent – permit some chaos effects. When mages look at the four elements, they see a decomposition of the eternal æther. The opposite of this æther is the void, which is defined by chaos. Chaos is the absence of the essential ætheric elements. More importantly, it’s the absence of any principle for the ordering of those elements. It’s an absence of the kind of balance that makes life possible.

The spell user must make an attack roll for each bolt, but it is treated as a single action. Each bolt causes 4D damage. The caster then rolls once on the Unleashed Chaos Table, regardless of his success in hitting the target.
