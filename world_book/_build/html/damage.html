Damage¶

Injury can come from all sides. This chapter describes two methods of figuring out how hurt a character, plus a few sources of damage other than weapons. Generally, the OpenD6 Fantasy rules can be used with some modifications. See the OpenD6 Fantasy, “Damage” chapter for the basics. There are some important additions.

Damage and Wound Levels¶

Generally, the OpenD6 Fantasy rules can be used with some modifications. See OpenD6 Fantasy, “Damage”, Damage & Wound Levels.

There is an important exception to the way death is handled. The following is a slight revision to the Fantasy “UNCONSCIOUSNESS & DEATH” section.

UNCONSCIOUSNESS & DEATH¶

When the character’s Body Points reach zero, the character is dying. They will linger in this state for a few minutes. At the end of each minute, the player makes a Physique roll against a difficulty equal to the number of minutes the character has been dead.

Sufficient medical aid to restore even one Body Point can rescue the imperiled character.

(In the case of poisons or other attacks that have lingering effects, the character may endure body point loss and return to the dying state.)

The character can be revived but he loses 1D from all of his skills, though the skill cannot go below the attribute’s die code.

Characters who have been dismembered, had their head removed, or reached below zero Body Points cannot be recovered or reanimated: their spirit will be drifting to another realm. (It typically takes a month or more to settle in a new realm, and in the ensuring period, any memories will have faded to vague feelings.)

Magic Fatigue¶

Anyone attempting an enchantment or a miracle will need Fatigue points. This starts at a value based on the a Magic skill or Miracle skill roll.

This measures the number of enchantments or miracles the character can perform before collapsing. There are two changes:

  • Attempting an enchantment or miracle costs 1 fatigue point. This is the Link Phase of the enchantment or miracle. Failed links and successful links have the same cost.

  • Working the enchantment or miracle costs 1 fatigue point per round. This is the Effect Phase.

  • Performing recovery practices and rituals restores the points. See the Healing section for details on fatigue point recovery.

A character with zero remaining fatigue points is unable to perform any magic or miracles safely. Each attempt at a link with zero fatigue points will cost 1 body; this can lead to the character becoming incapacitated. A critical failure with zero fatigue points results in being stunned and requiring healing (or time) to recover as well as losing 1 body.

If the link succeeds when a character has zero fatigue points, the enchantment or miracle effect will cost an additional 1 body per round the enchantment is maintained. The enchantment will end if the character becomes incapacitated.
